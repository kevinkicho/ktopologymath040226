Equation of State & CPL
The dark energy equation of state w = p/ρ determines expansion history. A cosmological constant has w = −1 exactly. Deviations signal new physics.
CPL parametrization:
w(a) = w₀ + wₐ(1−a)
Chevallier-Polarski-Linder form. a=1/(1+z) is the scale factor. At z=0, w=w₀; at high z, w→w₀+wₐ.
Hubble parameter:
H²(z)/H₀² = Ωm(1+z)³ + Ωde·f(z)
f(z) = (1+z)^(3(1+w₀+wₐ))·e^(−3wₐz/(1+z))
Key constraints (2024):
  • Planck 2018: w₀ = −1.03 ± 0.03
  • DESI 2024: w₀ = −0.99 ± 0.05
  • DESI+CMB+BAO: w₀wₐ tension with Λ at 3.9σ
Dark energy models:
  • Quintessence: scalar field, V(φ)∝φ⁻ⁿ, w > −1, thawing/freezing
  • Phantom field: w < −1, kinetic term wrong sign, leads to Big Rip
  • k-essence: non-canonical kinetic term
  • Axion: ultralight, fuzzy dark energy
Big Rip (w<−1): scale factor diverges in finite time t_rip = t₀ + 2/(3|1+w|H₀√Ωde). For w=−1.2, t_rip−t₀ ≈ 22 Gyr.

Type Ia Supernovae
SNe Ia are standardizable candles — not intrinsically identical, but their peak luminosity correlates tightly with decline rate (Phillips relation, 1993). After correction, σ ≈ 0.15 mag.
Distance modulus:
μ = 5 log₁₀(d_L / 10 pc)
Luminosity distance d_L = (1+z)·d_C where d_C = (c/H₀)∫dz/E(z).
Phillips relation:
M_B = −19.3 + 0.784(Δm₁₅−1.1)
1998 Discovery:
  • Perlmutter (SCP) & Riess/Schmidt (HZT) independently found SNe Ia at z~0.5 appeared ~0.25 mag fainter than expected
  • Conclusion: expansion is accelerating, Ω_Λ ≈ 0.7
  • Nobel Prize 2011: Perlmutter, Riess, Schmidt
Hubble Tension (2024):
  • Planck CMB: H₀ = 67.4 ± 0.5
  • SH0ES (Cepheids+SNe): H₀ = 73.0 ± 1.0
  • Tension: ~5.0σ — highly significant
  • Early dark energy, interacting dark energy, or systematics?
Distance ladder: Geometric parallax → Cepheid PLR → SNe Ia → H₀. Each rung multiplies uncertainties. JWST Cepheid re-analysis (2023) confirms SH0ES with reduced scatter.

Baryon Acoustic Oscillations
Before recombination (z~1100), photon-baryon plasma oscillated as sound waves. At recombination these waves froze, leaving a characteristic scale — the sound horizon r_s ≈ 147 Mpc — imprinted in galaxy clustering.
Sound horizon:
r_s = ∫₀^t_rec c_s dt/a = ∫₀^z_rec c_s dz/H(z)
c_s = c/√(3(1 + 3Ωb/4Ωγ))
The BAO peak in ξ(r) at ~100 h⁻¹ Mpc is a standard ruler — its angular size or redshift separation measures D_A(z) or H(z) directly.
Survey timeline:
  • 2dFGRS (2005): first hint of BAO
  • SDSS (2005, Eisenstein et al.): first 3.4σ detection
  • BOSS DR12 (2016): 1% D_A precision
  • eBOSS DR16 (2020): quasars at z~1.5
  • DESI 2024: 5.7M galaxies, best BAO yet
  • Euclid (2023–): 1.5 billion galaxy survey
S₈ tension:
Weak lensing surveys (DES, HSC, KiDS) measure S₈ = σ₈√(Ωm/0.3) ≈ 0.76, while Planck CMB predicts S₈ ≈ 0.83. ~2–3σ tension — possible sign of modified gravity or massive neutrinos.

The Cosmological Constant Problem
Quantum field theory predicts every field contributes zero-point energy to the vacuum. Summing all modes up to the Planck scale gives a vacuum energy density 120 orders of magnitude larger than observed — "the worst prediction in physics."
QFT estimate:
ρ_vac ≈ M_Pl⁴/(16π²) ≈ 2×10⁷⁴ GeV⁴
ρ_obs ≈ (2.3×10⁻³ eV)⁴ ≈ 2×10⁻¹¹ GeV⁴
The Weinberg bound (1987):
Anthropic argument: if |Λ| ≳ 100×ρ_matter at matter domination, galaxies cannot form. This predicts Λ within ~2 orders of ρ_matter — the first anthropic prediction confirmed by observation.
Proposed solutions:
  • SUSY: cancels bosons vs fermions, but still ΔΛ ∝ M_SUSY⁴ (60 orders off)
  • String landscape: 10⁵⁰⁰ vacua, anthropic selection
  • Sequestering: local/global separation of vacuum energy
  • Degravitation: vacuum energy doesn't gravitate
  • Self-tuning: dynamical cancellation mechanism
Λ = 8πG ρ_vac/c⁴. Current value: Λ ≈ 1.1×10⁻⁵² m⁻². Equivalent energy scale: (2.3 meV)⁴ — the scale of neutrino masses. Coincidence?

Next-Generation Dark Energy Surveys
Active / Recent:
  • DESI: 5000-fiber spectrograph, 14,000 deg², 40M galaxy spectra by 2026. Year-1 (2024): 5.7M spectra, best BAO to date.
  • Euclid: ESA space telescope (launched Jul 2023), 1.5B galaxies, weak lensing + spectroscopic BAO. DR1: 2024.
Upcoming:
  • Vera Rubin / LSST: first light 2024, 10-yr cadence, photometric SNe Ia at high-z, weak lensing tomography
  • Roman Space Telescope: grism spectroscopy, high-z SNe Ia, microlensing, launch ~2026
  • CMB-S4: next-gen CMB, lensing + BBN, constrains early dark energy
  • LISA / ET: gravitational wave standard sirens — independent H₀ probe
Figure of Merit (FoM):
FoM = 1/√det(Cov(w₀,wₐ))
Higher FoM = tighter constraints in w₀-wₐ plane. DESI Year-5 + Euclid combination: FoM > 500 (compared to Planck alone ~50).
21cm intensity mapping (CHIME, HIRAX, SKA): maps HI emission across z=0.8–2.5, cross-correlated with galaxy surveys for BAO. Independent of galaxy formation biases.
