EPR Paradox (1935)

Einstein, Podolsky, and Rosen argued that quantum mechanics is incomplete. If two particles interact and then fly apart, measuring one instantly determines the other — no matter the separation.

$$|\Psi^-\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\bigl(|\uparrow\downarrow\rangle - |\downarrow\uparrow\rangle\bigr)$$

This is the singlet state: total spin zero. If Alice measures spin-up along any axis, Bob must measure spin-down along the same axis — instantaneously.

Entanglement

A state is entangled if it cannot be written as a product $|\psi_A\rangle \otimes |\psi_B\rangle$. The singlet is maximally entangled: measuring either qubit collapses the joint state.

$P(+,+|\theta_A,\theta_B) = \tfrac{1}{2}\sin^2\!\tfrac{\theta_A-\theta_B}{2}$

Hidden Variables

Einstein's "locality + realism" intuition: the correlations must be explained by hidden variables $\lambda$ pre-programmed at the source — like a pair of gloves sent to opposite cities. Measuring one (left glove) instantly "determines" the other (right glove), but no spooky action occurs.

Bell (1964) proved this intuition is wrong.

Bell's Theorem

Any local hidden variable (LHV) theory must satisfy the CHSH inequality:

$$|S| = |\langle AB\rangle - \langle AB'\rangle + \langle A'B\rangle + \langle A'B'\rangle| \leq 2$$

Quantum mechanics predicts (for the singlet state):

$$E(\theta_A - \theta_B) = -\cos(\theta_A - \theta_B)$$
$$S_{\max} = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.828$$

Achieved at $a=0°$, $a'=45°$, $b=22.5°$, $b'=67.5°$. This Tsirelson bound exceeds the classical limit by $\sqrt{2}$, ruling out all LHV theories.

Bell Test Timeline

  • Clauser 1972 — First experimental Bell test. Violated classical bound but had loopholes.
  • Aspect 1982 — Added fast electro-optic switching, closing the locality loophole.
  • Zeilinger 1997 — Entangled photons over 400 m; satellite experiments to 1200 km.
  • Hensen 2015 — First loophole-free Bell test (nitrogen-vacancy centers, 1.3 km apart).
  • 2022 Nobel — Aspect, Clauser, Zeilinger awarded Nobel Prize in Physics.

Loopholes

Locality loophole: Alice and Bob's settings must be chosen after the photons leave the source, so no light-speed signal can carry the information. Aspect's fast switching closes this.

Detection loophole: If detectors miss too many photons, the detected subset might be biased. Hensen's 2015 experiment used near-unity detection efficiency to close both loopholes simultaneously.

$\eta > \frac{2(\sqrt{2}-1)}{1} \approx 82.8\%$

Minimum detection efficiency needed for a loophole-free test.

GHZ State

Greenberger, Horne, Zeilinger (1989) found a deterministic contradiction with local hidden variables — no statistical argument needed.

$$|GHZ\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\bigl(|000\rangle + |111\rangle\bigr)$$

QM Predictions

For the GHZ state, quantum mechanics predicts:

$X_A X_B X_C = +1$
$X_A Y_B Y_C = -1$
$Y_A X_B Y_C = -1$
$Y_A Y_B X_C = -1$

LHV Contradiction

Multiplying all four equations: $(X_AY_AX_AY_A)(X_BY_BY_A X_B)(X_CY_CY_CX_C) = +1\cdot(-1)^3 = -1$. But each $X_i^2=Y_i^2=1$, so the left side is $+1$. Thus $+1=-1$ — a logical contradiction! LHV must be wrong.

Mermin's "Impossible Boxes"

Mermin phrased this as three boxes each with buttons 1 and 2 and lights R/G. Whenever all press button 1: even number of R lights. Whenever one presses 1 and two press 2: odd number of R lights. No pre-programmed boxes can satisfy both rules — yet QM does.

CHSH Game

Alice receives bit $x\in\{0,1\}$, Bob receives $y\in\{0,1\}$, each outputs $a,b\in\{0,1\}$. They win if:

$$a \oplus b = x \wedge y$$

No communication allowed after receiving inputs. Classical strategies cannot exceed 75% win rate — one of the four input pairs always loses.

CHSH Inequality

$$|\langle AB\rangle + \langle AB'\rangle + \langle A'B\rangle - \langle A'B'\rangle| \leq 2$$

Classical (LHV): $\leq 2$. Quantum (Tsirelson 1980): $\leq 2\sqrt{2}$. No-signaling (PR boxes): $\leq 4$.

Quantum Strategy

Share a singlet $|\Psi^-\rangle$. Alice measures at $0°$ (x=0) or $45°$ (x=1). Bob at $22.5°$ (y=0) or $67.5°$ (y=1). Win probability:

$$P_{\rm win} = \cos^2\!\tfrac{\pi}{8} = \tfrac{1}{2}(1+\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}) \approx 85.36\%$$

Tsirelson's Theorem (1980)

The quantum maximum $2\sqrt{2}$ cannot be exceeded by any quantum strategy — it is the tight upper bound for quantum mechanics. The no-signaling bound of 4 (achieved by hypothetical PR boxes) exceeds quantum mechanics, showing QM is not the most nonlocal theory consistent with relativity.

Device-Independent Cryptography

A measured CHSH value $S > 2$ certifies entanglement without trusting the devices. The violation directly bounds Eve's information, enabling device-independent quantum key distribution (DI-QKD).

Three Major Loopholes

Locality loophole: Alice and Bob's settings must be chosen after the particles leave the source, spacelike-separated. Aspect 1982 partially closed this with fast switching.

Detection loophole: Low detector efficiency means only a biased subset of pairs is detected (fair sampling assumption). Requires $\eta > 82.8\%$.

Freedom-of-choice loophole: The random number generators choosing measurement settings could be correlated with the hidden variables. BIG Bell Test 2018 used 100,000 human choices.

Key Experiments

  • Clauser 1972 — First Bell test, violated LHV but both loopholes open.
  • Aspect 1982 — Fast switching, $S=2.697\pm0.015$. Locality loophole reduced.
  • Weihs 1998 — Spacelike separation enforced, locality loophole closed.
  • Rowe 2001 — Ion trap, detection loophole closed (but not locality).
  • Hensen 2015 — NV centers 1.3 km apart, both loopholes closed simultaneously. $p < 3.9\times10^{-5}$.
  • Giustina/Shalm 2015 — Optical, loophole-free with high-efficiency detectors.
  • BIG Bell Test 2018 — 100,000 humans generated random bits for settings worldwide.

Hensen 2015 Significance

$p < 3.9 \times 10^{-5}$

Equivalent to: if LHV were true, the probability of the observed result is less than 1 in 25,000. Combined with spacelike separation of 1.3 km, this is the most stringent test of local realism to date.

GHZ Nonlocality Game

Three players Alice, Bob, Charlie. Referee sends bits $s_A, s_B, s_C$ with $s_A \oplus s_B \oplus s_C = 0$. Each outputs $a, b, c \in \{0,1\}$. Win condition:

$$a \oplus b \oplus c = s_A \vee s_B \vee s_C$$

All-zero input $(0,0,0)$: must have $a\oplus b\oplus c=0$. Any input with two 1s: must have $a\oplus b\oplus c=1$.

Classical Impossibility

Any deterministic strategy assigns fixed parities. Let $p_A, p_B, p_C \in \{0,1\}$. For $(0,0,0)$: need $p_A\oplus p_B\oplus p_C=0$. For each pair-of-ones case, need parity 1. Multiplying all four parity constraints: $0 = 1$ — contradiction. No classical strategy wins all cases.

GHZ Quantum Strategy

Prepare $|GHZ\rangle = (|000\rangle + |111\rangle)/\sqrt{2}$. Measure in $X$ basis if $s_i=0$, in $Y$ basis if $s_i=1$. QM predictions are:

$X_AX_BX_C|GHZ\rangle: +1$
$X_AY_BY_C|GHZ\rangle: -1$

Remapping $\pm 1 \to \{0,1\}$: quantum always wins — deterministically, with no statistical argument needed.

GHZ vs CHSH

CHSH requires statistics (many rounds) to see the violation. GHZ produces a logical contradiction: a single correct measurement set would simultaneously prove $+1 = -1$ for LHV. This is Mermin's "all-or-nothing" nonlocality.

Quantum Steering

Intermediate between entanglement and Bell nonlocality. Alice and Bob share an entangled state. Alice measures and remotely prepares different ensembles of Bob's states — even if Bob does not trust Alice's device.

An assemblage $\{\sigma_{a|x}\}$ describes Bob's conditional states for Alice's measurement $x$, outcome $a$:

$$\sigma_{a|x} = \text{tr}_A\bigl[(M_{a|x}\otimes I)\rho_{AB}\bigr]$$

Steering is unsteerable if and only if $\sigma_{a|x} = \sum_\lambda p(\lambda)p(a|x,\lambda)\rho_\lambda^B$ (a local hidden state model).

EPR-Steering Inequality

$$F_n = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{k=1}^n \langle A_k \otimes B_k \rangle \leq C_{\rm LHS}$$

For $n=2$ incompatible measurements: $C_{\rm LHS} = 1/\sqrt{2} \approx 0.707$. Quantum mechanics achieves 1, violating the bound.

Kochen-Specker Theorem

No assignment of definite values $v(A)\in\{0,1\}$ to all quantum observables can be consistent with the algebraic structure of QM. Specifically: in $\geq 3$ dimensions, no non-contextual hidden variable theory can reproduce all quantum predictions.

$$\text{No coloring of } S^2 \text{ exists s.t. any orthonormal basis sums to 1}$$

KCBS Inequality

Klyachko-Can-Binicioğlu-Shumovsky (2008): 5 observables $A_1,\ldots,A_5$ in a cycle where adjacent pairs are compatible:

$$\sum_{i=1}^5 \langle A_i A_{i+1}\rangle \geq -3 \quad \text{(classical)}$$
$$\sum_{i=1}^5 \langle A_i A_{i+1}\rangle = -\sqrt{5} \approx -2.236 \quad \text{(quantum)}$$

Quantum violates the classical KCBS bound, proving contextuality with just 5 observables.

Contextuality as a Resource

Bravyi-Gottesman-Kitaev (2005): Contextuality is the key resource for universal quantum computation beyond stabilizer circuits. Magic state distillation requires contextuality. This connects foundational nonlocality tests to practical quantum advantage.

Ekert 1991 Protocol (E91)

Artur Ekert proposed using entangled pairs and Bell inequality violation to distribute secret keys. Unlike BB84, security does not rely on trusting quantum devices.

  • Alice and Bob each receive one qubit of a shared entangled pair
  • Each independently chooses a random measurement basis
  • Correlated results (same basis) form the raw key
  • Discordant results (different basis) test the CHSH inequality
  • CHSH violation $S > 2$ certifies no eavesdropper obtained information

Device-Independent Security

Acín et al. (2007) proved that a measured CHSH value $S$ bounds Eve's information:

$$r \geq 1 - h\!\left(\tfrac{1+\sqrt{(S/2)^2-1}}{2}\right) - h(e)$$

where $h$ is the binary entropy function and $e$ is the quantum bit error rate (QBER). For $S = 2\sqrt{2}$ (no noise): $r \to 1$ bit/pair.

BB84 vs E91

  • BB84: Prepare-and-measure; security requires trusted devices; proven secure under coherent attacks.
  • E91: Entanglement-based; security certified by Bell violation; fully device-independent (no trust in hardware).

Randomness Certification

A measured CHSH value $S$ also certifies randomness: the outputs cannot be predicted by any adversary limited by quantum mechanics, even if they built the devices. This enables device-independent random number generation from Bell tests.

$$H_{\min}(A|E) \geq 1 - \log_2\!\left(1 + \sqrt{2 - \tfrac{S^2}{4}}\right)$$

Current Experimental State

Full DI-QKD requires loophole-free Bell tests with high key rates — challenging experimentally. Current experiments achieve "semi-device-independent" QKD. The 2022 Nobel work (Aspect, Clauser, Zeilinger) laid the experimental foundation. Groups at Delft, Vienna, and NIST are pursuing loophole-free DI-QKD over metropolitan distances for the quantum internet.

2022 Nobel Prize in Physics

Alain Aspect (Institut d'Optique, France), John F. Clauser (USA), and Anton Zeilinger (University of Vienna, Austria) were awarded the Nobel Prize "for experiments with entangled photons, establishing the violation of Bell inequalities and pioneering quantum information science."

Their work proved conclusively that nature is nonlocal at the quantum level: there are no hidden variables that can explain quantum correlations while respecting Einstein's locality. This foundational result also underpins quantum cryptography, quantum teleportation, and quantum computing.

Prize amount: 10 million Swedish kronor (shared)
