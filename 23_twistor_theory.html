Penrose's Original Vision: Spacetime from Twistors
The fundamental duality: a point $x \in \mathbb{CM}$ (complexified Minkowski space) corresponds to a projective line $\hat{x} \cong \mathbb{CP}^1$ in projective twistor space $\mathbb{PT} \cong \mathbb{CP}^3$. Conversely, a single twistor $Z^\alpha = (\omega^A, \pi_{A'})$ determines an α-plane — a self-dual totally null 2-plane in $\mathbb{CM}$ — via the incidence relation $\omega^A = ix^{A\dot A}\pi_{\dot A}$. On the real slice (Minkowski space $\mathbb{M}$), a twistor with $Z^\alpha\bar Z_\alpha = 0$ (a point in $\mathbb{PN}$, the "real" part of $\mathbb{PT}$) corresponds to a null ray — the real intersection of the α-plane with $\mathbb{M}$. The α-planes fanning out from P trace the future null cone.

Robinson Congruence — The Most Beautiful Twistor Geometry
A single point $Z \in \mathbb{PN}$ (real projective twistor space) corresponds to a Robinson congruence: a 3-parameter family of null rays in Minkowski space $\mathbb{M}$ forming a twisted, shear-free congruence. The congruence has the structure of a Hopf fibration $S^3 \to S^2$: the null rays at fixed time slice form a family of circles (or a single line) that fills 3-space, with each pair of circles linked exactly once. Rotating the phase of $Z$ in $\mathbb{PN}$ smoothly rotates the entire congruence. At any moment, the congruence defines a principal null direction of a radiative field. This object — a 4D twisting pattern of light rays — was Penrose's original motivation for inventing twistors: to make null geometry primary.

Penrose's Nonlinear Graviton Construction (1976)
In flat spacetime, twistor space is $\mathbb{PT} \cong \mathbb{CP}^3$ with its standard complex structure. When spacetime has a self-dual gravitational field (positive-helicity gravitons), Penrose showed that the complex structure of twistor space is deformed — the lines $\mathbb{CP}^1 \subset \mathbb{PT}$ corresponding to spacetime points are bent away from straight lines in homogeneous coordinates. Concretely: choose a Kodaira deformation of the complex structure, parameterized by $\Psi_{ABCD}$ (the self-dual Weyl tensor). The deformed space $\mathbb{PT}'$ encodes the full non-perturbative self-dual gravitational field. This is the nonlinear graviton: twistor space geometrically encodes gravity without approximation. It was the first fully non-perturbative construction in quantum gravity theory. The grid below shows the deformed complex structure. Each cell represents a coordinate patch; the shearing shows how holomorphic coordinates rotate under the deformation $H^1(\mathbb{PT}, T\mathbb{PT}(-4))$.

Twistor String Theory — Witten 2003
Edward Witten's 2003 paper "Perturbative Gauge Theory as a String Theory in Twistor Space" launched the modern on-shell amplitude revolution. The key insight: gluon scattering amplitudes in $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM correspond to D1-brane instantons wrapping holomorphic curves in supertwistor space $\mathbb{CP}^{3|4}$. A degree-$d$ genus-$g$ curve computes N$^{d-1}$MHV amplitudes:
  • $d=1$ (line, genus 0): MHV amplitudes — the Parke-Taylor formula $\langle ij\rangle^4/\prod\langle kl\rangle$
  • $d=2$ (conic, genus 0): NMHV amplitudes (one negative-helicity gluon "extra")
  • $d=3$ (cubic): N²MHV amplitudes
  • Genus $g > 0$: loop corrections
The holomorphic curve is a map $f: \Sigma_{g,d} \to \mathbb{CP}^{3|4}$ where each external gluon inserts a vertex operator on the worldsheet $\Sigma$. The canvas shows the worldsheet mapped into a 2D projection of supertwistor space.
