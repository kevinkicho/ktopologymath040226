CMB Hawking Points

δT/T|ring < σbackground
P(detect|CCC) vs P(detect|ΛCDM)

CCC prediction (Penrose & Gurzadyan, 2010): When supermassive black holes merge in a previous aeon, they emit isotropic bursts of gravitational waves. These propagate to the conformally rescaled Big Bang, imprinting as circles of anomalously low temperature variance in the CMB. Penrose calls these Hawking points — the final Hawking radiation burst of ancient black holes, visible as rings in our sky.

Statistical controversy

Gurzadyan & Penrose claimed detection in WMAP data (2010). Three independent groups (Wehus & Eriksen; Moss, Scott & Zibin; Hajian) rebutted: the CMB variance circles fall within the expected range of a standard ΛCDM Gaussian random field. The crucial point is that a random Gaussian field on S² naturally produces such features; CCC rings must be statistically anomalous beyond chance.

Planck era status

Penrose, An, Meissner, & Gurzadyan (2020) claimed 30+ Hawking points in Planck data at >99% significance, with a clustering of low-variance spots around specific centers. Critics dispute the trial factor correction. No consensus detection; the claim remains scientifically open but controversial.

• A confirmed detection would be the first evidence for physics before the Big Bang
• Distinguishing feature: rings should be correlated in radius with BH mass in previous aeon
• Future: CMB-S4, Simons Observatory will provide the definitive dataset

Penrose-Hameroff (1994–present): Quantum superpositions in tubulin dimers (the protein subunits of microtubules in neurons) collapse via OR when EG = ℏ/𝜏. The collapse is orchestrated by microtubule-associated proteins (MAPs), making it non-computable in Penrose's sense — the source of conscious experience.

OR condition

EG is the gravitational self-energy of the mass-difference distribution between the two arms of the superposition. For a tubulin dimer (~10⁻²² kg) displaced ~1 nm, 𝜏 ~ 10⁵ seconds — far too slow for a neuron. Hameroff argues coherence across many tubulins reduces 𝜏 to ~25 ms (gamma oscillation timescale).

Penrose's Twistor Programme

ωᴮ = ixᴮᴮ’ πA' (incidence relation)
H¹(PT, 𝒪(-n-2)) ⟷ helicity-n/2 fields

Twistor space (Penrose, 1967): Replace spacetime as the fundamental arena with twistor space 𝕋, a complex 4D space ℂ⁴. A spacetime point x corresponds to a Riemann sphere CP¹ (a "line") in projective twistor space PT = CP³. A massless particle's worldline becomes a point in twistor space.

Penrose Transform

Massless fields (Maxwell, linearized gravity) are encoded in sheaf cohomology classes on regions of PT. The Penrose transform provides an exact correspondence: H¹(PT⁺, 𝒪(-n-2)) ↔ zero-rest-mass fields of helicity n/2 satisfying field equations in Minkowski space. This encodes field equations geometrically without writing down differential equations explicitly.

• Amplituhedron (Arkani-Hamed & Trnka, 2013): scattering amplitudes as volumes of geometric objects — twistor geometry at heart

Black Hole Information Paradox

TH = ℏc³ / (8πGkBM)
SBH = A / (4Gℏ) = 4πGM² / (ℏc)

Hawking (1974): Black holes emit thermal radiation with temperature TH ∝ 1/M. The radiation is perfectly thermal — no information about the infalling matter escapes. After complete evaporation, information is lost. This violates unitarity of quantum mechanics.

Page Curve

Don Page (1993): if evolution is unitary, the von Neumann entropy S of the radiation must decrease after the Page time tPage ~ M³. Early radiation is nearly thermal; late radiation must be entangled with early radiation to purify the state. The "island formula" (Penington; Almheiri et al., 2019) reproduces the Page curve via replica wormholes in the gravitational path integral.

Competing resolutions

• Complementarity (Susskind & 't Hooft): infaller sees no drama; outside observer sees info encoded in Hawking radiation. No contradiction if neither can compare notes.
• Firewall paradox (AMPS 2012): complementarity + monogamy of entanglement ⇒ drama at horizon (firewall)
• Island formula S = min(A/4G + Smatter on island) — gravitational entropy includes contributions from islands behind horizon
• Penrose's view: OR resolves the paradox — the collapse of the quantum state during OR is a non-unitary, physical process; information is genuinely lost in black hole evaporation, consistent with OR

Loop Quantum Gravity

A = 8πγℓP² ∑j √(j(j+1))
V ~ ℓP³ (discrete volume quanta)

LQG (Rovelli, Smolin, Ashtekar, 1986–present): Spacetime itself is discrete at the Planck scale ℓP ~ 10⁻³⁵ m. Area eigenvalues are discrete: A = 8πγℓP² ∑ √(ji(ji+1)), where ji are half-integer spin quantum numbers labeling spin network edges. The Barbero-Immirzi parameter γ ~ 0.274 is fixed by black hole entropy.

Spin foams

The covariant formulation describes spacetime history as a 2-complex (spin foam) with vertex amplitudes W(jf, ie). The EPRL model gives a well-defined vertex amplitude relating to the Regge action in the large-j limit, connecting LQG to discrete path integrals over geometries.
