Maxwell's Equations

Gauss's law (electric)
$\nabla\cdot\mathbf{E}=\dfrac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0}$
Gauss's law (magnetic)
$\nabla\cdot\mathbf{B}=0$
Faraday's law
$\nabla\times\mathbf{E}=-\dfrac{\partial\mathbf{B}}{\partial t}$
Ampère-Maxwell law
$\nabla\times\mathbf{B}=\mu_0\mathbf{J}+\mu_0\varepsilon_0\dfrac{\partial\mathbf{E}}{\partial t}$

4-Potential Form

Field tensor
$F_{\mu\nu}=\partial_\mu A_\nu - \partial_\nu A_\mu$
Maxwell equations
$\partial^\mu F_{\mu\nu}=\mu_0 J_\nu$
Bianchi identity
$\partial_{[\lambda}F_{\mu\nu]}=0$
Gauge transformation
$A_\mu \to A_\mu + \partial_\mu\chi$
Integral form (Gauss):
$\oint_S \mathbf{E}\cdot d\mathbf{A} = Q_\text{enc}/\varepsilon_0$

The Gaussian circle drawn on the canvas encloses charges. Field lines crossing outward count as positive flux.

Wave Equations

Wave equation
$\nabla^2\mathbf{E}=\mu_0\varepsilon_0\dfrac{\partial^2\mathbf{E}}{\partial t^2}$
Speed of light
$c=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}}\approx 3\times10^8\ \text{m/s}$
Poynting vector
$\mathbf{S}=\dfrac{1}{\mu_0}\mathbf{E}\times\mathbf{B}$
Energy density
$u=\dfrac{\varepsilon_0 E^2}{2}+\dfrac{B^2}{2\mu_0}$
Dispersion relation:
$\omega = ck$,   $\lambda = 2\pi/k$

Polarization states:
Linear: E fixed in a plane
Circular: $\mathbf{E}$ rotates; $|E|$ constant
Elliptical: general superposition

E (red) and B (cyan) are always perpendicular to each other and to k.

Gauge Theory

Lagrangian density
$\mathcal{L}=-\dfrac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}$
Minimal coupling
$p_\mu \to p_\mu - qA_\mu$
Lorentz force
$\mathbf{F}=q(\mathbf{E}+\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B})$
U(1) symmetry
$\psi\to e^{i\alpha(x)}\psi$
Cyclotron motion (B only):
$\omega_c = qB/m$,   $r = mv/qB$

E×B drift:
$\mathbf{v}_d = \dfrac{\mathbf{E}\times\mathbf{B}}{B^2}$

Noether's theorem:
$U(1)$ gauge symmetry $\Rightarrow$ conservation of electric charge.
Aharonov-Bohm effect:
Outside the solenoid $\mathbf{B}=0$, yet the vector potential $\mathbf{A}\neq 0$. Electron paths acquire a phase:
$\Delta\varphi=\dfrac{q}{\hbar}\oint \mathbf{A}\cdot d\mathbf{l}=\dfrac{q\Phi_B}{\hbar}$
Observable interference despite zero local field — $\mathbf{A}$ is physically real.

Maxwell as 2-Forms

1-form potential
$A = -\phi\,dt + A_x\,dx + A_y\,dy + A_z\,dz$
2-form field strength
$F = dA \;\;\Rightarrow\;\; F_{\mu\nu}=\partial_\mu A_\nu - \partial_\nu A_\mu$
Bianchi identity (free)
$dF = 0$   encodes $\nabla\cdot B=0$ and Faraday
Source equation
$d{\star}F = {\star}J$   encodes Gauss + Ampère
Gauge invariance
$A \to A + d\chi$   leaves $F=dA$ unchanged (since $d^2=0$)
Hodge dual (EM duality)
${\star}F$: swap $\mathbf{E}\leftrightarrow\mathbf{B}$, dual Maxwell
Why 2-form?
$F$ has 6 independent components: $E_x,E_y,E_z,B_x,B_y,B_z$.
In 4D spacetime, a 2-form is a skew-symmetric rank-2 tensor — exactly the field tensor $F_{\mu\nu}$.

Self-dual condition:
${\star}F = iF$ (in Euclidean signature) singles out the EM wave — the 2-form is self-dual when $|\mathbf{E}|=c|\mathbf{B}|$ and $\mathbf{E}\perp\mathbf{B}$.

Lienard-Wiechert Potentials

Scalar potential
$\phi = \dfrac{q}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\dfrac{1}{R - \mathbf{R}\cdot\boldsymbol{\beta}}$
Vector potential
$\mathbf{A} = \dfrac{\mu_0 qc\,\boldsymbol{\beta}}{4\pi(R - \mathbf{R}\cdot\boldsymbol{\beta})}$
Radiation field (far zone)
$\mathbf{E}_\text{rad} \propto \dfrac{\hat{R}\times[(\hat{R}-\boldsymbol{\beta})\times\dot{\boldsymbol{\beta}}]}{(1-\hat{R}\cdot\boldsymbol{\beta})^3 R}$
Power angular distribution
$\dfrac{dP}{d\Omega} = \dfrac{q^2 a^2}{16\pi^2\varepsilon_0 c^3}\sin^2\theta$
Near field vs far field:
Near (Coulomb): $E \propto 1/r^2$ — carries no energy away.
Far (radiation): $E \propto 1/r$ — carries energy to infinity.

Larmor formula:
$P = \dfrac{q^2 a^2}{6\pi\varepsilon_0 c^3}$

Radiation is maximum perpendicular to acceleration ($\theta=90°$) and zero along the acceleration axis. Relativistic beaming: radiation is concentrated forward at $\theta\sim 1/\gamma$.

Photon from U(1) Symmetry

Free Dirac Lagrangian
$\mathcal{L}_0 = \bar\psi(i\gamma^\mu\partial_\mu - m)\psi$
Global U(1): $\psi\to e^{i\alpha}\psi$
$\mathcal{L}_0$ invariant. Noether: $\partial_\mu J^\mu=0$, $J^\mu=\bar\psi\gamma^\mu\psi$
Local U(1): $\psi\to e^{i\alpha(x)}\psi$
$\partial_\mu\psi \to e^{i\alpha}(\partial_\mu + i\partial_\mu\alpha)\psi$ — NOT invariant!
Fix: covariant derivative
$D_\mu = \partial_\mu + ieA_\mu$,   $A_\mu\to A_\mu - \tfrac{1}{e}\partial_\mu\alpha$
Full QED Lagrangian
$\mathcal{L} = \bar\psi(i\gamma^\mu D_\mu - m)\psi - \tfrac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}$
Why massless photon?
A mass term $\tfrac{1}{2}m^2 A_\mu A^\mu$ breaks gauge invariance. Local U(1) symmetry forces the photon to remain massless.

Minimal coupling:
$p_\mu \to p_\mu - eA_\mu$ encodes all EM interaction.

Charge conservation:
Global $U(1)$ $\Rightarrow$ $\partial_\mu J^\mu=0$ (Noether). This is the conserved electric charge $Q=\int J^0 d^3x$.

Optics from Maxwell

Boundary conditions
$E_{1t}=E_{2t}$,   $D_{1n}=D_{2n}$
$B_{1n}=B_{2n}$,   $H_{1t}=H_{2t}$
Snell's law (from phase match)
$n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2$
Total internal reflection
$\theta_c = \arcsin(n_2/n_1)$   (evanescent wave: $E\propto e^{-\kappa z}$)
Malus's law
$I = I_0\cos^2\theta$
Faraday rotation
$\beta = \mathcal{V} B d$   (Verdet constant $\mathcal{V}$)
Geometric optics limit:
When $\lambda\ll$ object size, Maxwell gives straight rays. Diffraction appears when $\lambda\sim$ slit width.

Evanescent wave:
Beyond critical angle, the transmitted wave decays exponentially. Tunneling (frustrated TIR) couples it back — basis of fiber optics couplers and TIRF microscopy.

Faraday rotation:
A magnetic field along the propagation direction breaks time-reversal symmetry, rotating the polarization plane. Used in optical isolators.

Casimir Effect

Zero-point energy per mode
$E_n = \tfrac{1}{2}\hbar\omega_n$
Allowed modes between plates
$k_n = \dfrac{n\pi}{d}$,   $\omega_n = ck_n$
Casimir pressure (renormalized)
$\dfrac{F}{A} = -\dfrac{\hbar c\,\pi^2}{240\,d^4}$
At $d=100\,\text{nm}$:
$F/A \approx -0.013\,\text{atm}$ (attractive!)
Mechanism:
Between the plates, only modes with $\lambda = 2d/n$ fit. Outside the plates, all modes exist. The mode density is lower between the plates, giving lower vacuum energy — the plates are pushed together.

Cosmological constant problem:
The quantum vacuum energy density (naive sum: $\sum\tfrac{1}{2}\hbar\omega$) diverges. Renormalized, the Casimir effect gives a finite, measured attractive force. But in cosmology, a non-zero vacuum energy would act as a cosmological constant $\Lambda$. Why $\Lambda$ is so small compared to quantum field theory predictions is one of the deepest open problems in physics.

Measured: Lamoreaux (1997), Mohideen & Roy (1998) confirmed $F\propto 1/d^4$.
