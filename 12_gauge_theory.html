β-function (1-loop)
$\mu\frac{d\alpha}{d\mu} = \beta(\alpha)$
QED: $\beta = +\frac{\alpha^2}{3\pi}N_f$
QCD: $\beta = -\frac{\alpha_s^2}{2\pi}(11-\frac{2N_f}{3})$
The negative QCD β-function (for $N_f<17$) causes asymptotic freedom. Gross, Politzer & Wilczek won the 2004 Nobel Prize for this discovery.
SU(3) Color
Confinement: color flux lines collapse into a flux tube of constant tension $\sigma\approx 1$ GeV/fm. Energy grows linearly — quarks can never be isolated.

Gluon self-coupling: unlike photons, gluons carry color charge and interact with each other — causing the anti-screening that produces asymptotic freedom.
Yang-Mills Theory
Action

$= -\tfrac{1}{4}\int G^a_{\mu\nu}G^{a\mu\nu}d^4x$
β-function (1-loop)
$\beta(g)=-\dfrac{g^3}{16\pi^2}\!\left(\tfrac{11N_c-2n_f}{3}\right)$

For SU(3), $N_c=3$, $n_f=6$: $\beta_0 = 7$
String tension
$V(r) = -\dfrac{4\alpha_s}{3r} + \sigma r$

$\sigma \approx (0.44\,\mathrm{GeV})^2$
Lattice QCD confirms confinement numerically — the plaquette action reproduces the hadron spectrum at the 1–5% level. Quark-gluon plasma deconfines above $T_c\approx 150$ MeV.
Topology
Instanton: a self-dual field configuration in Euclidean $\mathbb{R}^4$ with winding number $Q=1$. It represents a tunneling event between topologically distinct vacua $|n\rangle\to|n{+}1\rangle$.

Strong CP problem: the $\theta$-term $\frac{\theta g^2}{32\pi^2}G\tilde{G}$ is CP-violating, yet experiments give $\theta < 10^{-10}$. Peccei-Quinn axions explain why.

Atiyah-Singer index theorem links the topological charge $Q$ to the number of zero-modes of the Dirac operator — connecting geometry, topology and quantum field theory.
Higgs Potential
SSB: when $\mu^2>0$, the minimum is at $|\phi|=v=\sqrt{\mu^2/2\lambda}$. The field acquires a vev breaking $U(1)\to\varnothing$.

Drag $\mu^2$ from negative (symmetric phase) to positive (broken phase) to see the Mexican hat form.

Glashow-Salam-Weinberg (1979 Nobel). The 3 Goldstone bosons are "eaten" by W±, Z, providing their longitudinal polarizations. The physical Higgs boson was found at the LHC in 2012.
Anomaly Type
ABJ Anomaly: the axial current $j^\mu_5=\bar\psi\gamma^\mu\gamma^5\psi$ is not conserved at one loop — the triangle diagram breaks the classical symmetry.

Slide the color count: the Standard Model anomalies cancel exactly when $N_c=3$.

Wilson's lattice: the gauge field lives on links $U_\mu(x)=e^{iagA_\mu}$. The plaquette action preserves exact gauge invariance on the lattice. As $\beta\to\infty$ (small $g$), the continuum limit is recovered.
