Precession Formula
$$\omega_a = \frac{eB}{m_\mu}\,a_\mu$$
$$a_\mu = \frac{\omega_a}{\omega_c}$$
The anomalous frequency $\omega_a = \omega_s - \omega_c$ is the measurable beat note between spin and cyclotron precession. Only the anomalous part of the magnetic moment survives.
Thomas Precession
$$\omega_s = \frac{eB}{m_\mu}\!\left(a_\mu + \frac{1}{\gamma}\right)$$
CBO (coherent betatron oscillation) at ~470 kHz modulates the decay positron count and is the dominant systematic. The electric focusing quadrupoles are shimmed to suppress radial-field imperfections to ppb level.
ρ Peak Position
The ρ(770) meson dominates the $e^+e^-\!\to\!\pi^+\pi^-$ cross-section and thus ~75% of the HVP integral. Even a 0.5% shift in this peak changes $a_\mu^{HVP}$ by ~4 units.
Dataset
CMD-3 2023 measured $\sigma(\pi^+\pi^-)$ ~2.5% higher than CMD-2 — shifting $a_\mu^{HVP}$ UP by ~9 units, potentially resolving the g-2 tension. But CMD-3 disagrees with BaBar ISR data. The HVP puzzle is unresolved.
Dispersion Relation
$$a_\mu^{HVP} = \frac{\alpha^2}{3\pi^2}\!\int_{m_\pi^2}^{\infty}\!\frac{K(s)}{s}\,\hat{\sigma}(s)\,ds$$
$K(s)$ is a known QED kernel weighting low-$s$ heavily — the ρ peak is the dominant region. This is why disagreements at the 1% level in $\sigma_{\pi\pi}$ propagate to the g-2 tension.
BMW Lattice QCD
BMW group (2020, PRL): $a_\mu^{HVP(lat)} = 7075(55)\!\times\!10^{-11}$, about +90 units above dispersive. If correct, the SM prediction rises and tension with experiment drops to ~1.5σ. But BMW disagrees with CMD-2/BaBar window quantities — the "BMW vs data" sub-puzzle.
Crivellin et al. 2020: internal consistency of electroweak precision data would be spoiled if BMW result is used — hinting dispersive may be correct.

NP Sensitivity
$$\Delta a_\mu \sim \frac{m_\mu^2}{\Lambda_{NP}^2}$$
A 4.2σ anomaly of $\Delta a_\mu \approx 2.5\!\times\!10^{-9}$ implies NP at $\Lambda_{NP}\sim\!O(100\text{ GeV}–1\text{ TeV})$ — precisely the LHC scale.
SUSY Loop
Smuon-neutralino (or sneutrino-chargino) loop contributes:
$a_\mu^{SUSY}\!\sim\!\frac{\alpha m_\mu^2\tan\beta}{m_{SUSY}^2}$
For $\tan\beta\!\sim\!40$, $m_{SUSY}\!\sim\!400$ GeV gives the right size.
The $(g-2)_\mu$ anomaly and the $(g-2)_e$ anomaly (opposite sign, Cs measurement) together disfavor simple one-loop NP — pointing toward lepton-nonuniversal NP or hadronic effects.
CP violation: an EDM at $10^{-21}$ would require CP phases $\delta_{CP}\!\sim\!10^{-3}$ in SUSY, or $O(1)$ in TeV-scale composite models.

Dirac 1928 predicted $g=2$ exactly. Kusch & Foley measured $g\neq 2$ in 1947. Schwinger explained the discrepancy with the one-loop QED correction in 1948.
Lamb Shift QED
$$\Delta E_{Lamb} = \frac{\alpha^5 m_e}{6\pi}\ln\!\frac{1}{\alpha^2}$$
Muonic hydrogen Lamb shift (Pohl et al. 2010, Nature): proton radius $r_p = 0.84087(39)$ fm — 7σ smaller than CODATA 2010 value from electronic hydrogen. Partially resolved by new H spectroscopy (0.833 fm) but not fully.
W Boson Mass
CDF 2022: $M_W = 80.4335(94)$ GeV — 7σ above SM prediction of 80.357 GeV. ATLAS 2023 and LHCb disagree with CDF. The tension may be experimental, but it dominated precision electroweak discussions.
Dark sector: a dark photon of mass $m_{A'}\!\sim\!10$–$100$ MeV could contribute $\Delta a_\mu\!\sim\!10^{-9}$ — the right size. Current dark photon searches exclude most parameter space, but dark Z and axion-like particles remain viable.
