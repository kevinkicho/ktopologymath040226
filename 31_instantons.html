Instantons cannot be seen in Minkowski space — they are tunneling trajectories between different vacuum sectors, connected through the barrier of the gauge potential energy.

The index of the Dirac operator in an instanton background equals the topological charge. For k=1: exactly one left-handed normalizable zero mode, zero right-handed.

The zero mode ψ₀(x) is localized on the instanton with the same scale ρ. It satisfies D̸ψ₀ = 0 — an exact eigenvalue at λ=0.

Sphaleron Solution

E_sph = (4π/g) · B(λ/g²) B ≈ 1.52 (Klinkhamer-Manton) E_sph ≈ 9 TeV (at T=0)

The sphaleron (Greek: "ready to fall") is an unstable static solution of the electroweak field equations, sitting at the top of the potential barrier between topological sectors.

At T ≈ 0: quantum tunneling through the barrier via instantons. Rate exponentially suppressed.

At T > Tsph ≈ 10 TeV (electroweak scale): thermal fluctuations drive the system over the sphaleron barrier. Rate becomes unsuppressed.

Γ_sph ~ T⁴ e^{−E_sph/T} (low T) Γ_sph ~ α_W⁴ T⁴ (high T) ΔB = ΔL = N_f per crossing

Baryogenesis: Sakharov conditions — (1) B violation ✓, (2) C and CP violation, (3) departure from thermal equilibrium. Electroweak baryogenesis uses sphaleron transitions at the electroweak phase transition.

Instanton Moduli Space

k=1, SU(2): 8 parameters total: − 4: center x₀ ∈ ℝ⁴ − 1: size ρ ∈ ℝ₊ − 3: orientation ∈ SU(2)/U(1) dim(ℳ_1) = 8·1 − 3 = 5

The moduli space ℳ_k is the space of all self-dual gauge field configurations with topological charge k, modulo gauge equivalence. It is a smooth manifold of dimension 8k−3 for SU(2).

The Yang-Mills action is constant along ℳ_k: S = 8π²k/g² for all points. This "flat direction" (valley) is the hallmark of BPS saturation.

Collective coordinates: z_i ≡ moduli (zero modes) Integrate over ℳ_k: ∫_{ℳ_k} [dz] e^{−S} J(z)

Physically, the zero modes of the instanton background are the Goldstone modes of the action — fluctuations that cost zero action. They must be integrated exactly (not in saddle-point approximation).

Multi-instanton: dim(ℳ_k) = 8k − 3 For k=2: 13 params (relative position, relative orientation...)

The ADHM construction (Atiyah-Drinfeld-Hitchin-Manin) gives an explicit algebraic parametrization of all instantons via linear algebra data on a vector space, revealing deep connections to algebraic geometry.
