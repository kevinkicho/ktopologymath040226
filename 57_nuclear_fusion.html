Fusion Reaction
NIF 2022 achieved the first ever $Q>1$ — 3.15 MJ out from 2.05 MJ in. The ignition region is shaded above the contour.
Lawson Criterion
$$n\tau_E T \geq 3\times10^{21}\,\text{keV} \cdot \text{s/m}^3$$
$$^2\text{H}+^3\text{H}\to^4\text{He}+n$$
Q = 17.6 MeV
All three factors must be simultaneously large: density $n$, confinement time $\tau_E$, and temperature $T$.
Rayleigh-Taylor instability grows at the ablation front, seeding mix that quenches ignition. NIF uses indirect drive (hohlraum X-rays) to reduce asymmetry.
RT Instability
$$\gamma_{RT} = \sqrt{A k g}$$
A = Atwood number, k = wavenumber, g = effective gravity. Higher k modes grow fastest but are stabilized by ablative flow at the surface.
Each point is an NIF implosion shot. The Dec 2022 shot (gold) crossed Q=1 for the first time.
Ignition Gain
$$Q = \frac{E_{fusion}}{E_{laser}}$$
NIF Dec 2022: $E_{laser}=2.05$ MJ, $E_{fusion}=3.15$ MJ → $Q\approx1.54$. Commercial power requires $Q\gtrsim100$.

D-T Reaction
$$^2\text{H}+^3\text{H}\to ^4\text{He}(3.5\,\text{MeV})+n(14.1\,\text{MeV})$$
Lawson (Triple Product)
$$n T \tau_E \geq 3\times10^{21}\,\text{keV}\cdot\text{m}^{-3}\text{s}$$

S-factor for D-T: $S(0)\approx 6.27\times10^{-2}$ MeV·b. Nearly constant near peak — tunneling factor dominates the energy dependence.

Burn Propagation
$$\alpha\text{-heating: }^4\text{He}(3.5\,\text{MeV})\text{ deposits in fuel}$$
Ignition condition: $\alpha$-particle energy deposition exceeds heat losses. Requires $\rho R_{hs} \gtrsim 0.3\,\text{g/cm}^2$.
Hohlraum: gold cylinder converts laser light to X-rays (indirect drive), smoothing illumination. Alternative: direct drive (ablator directly irradiated). Fast ignition (LFEX laser) uses separate ignition pulse.
Facilities: NIF (1.8 MJ), Omega (30 kJ, Rochester), SG-III (China, 200 kJ), ELI-NP (Europe).
Turbulence Drive
Critical threshold: ITG turbulence activates for $R/L_T \gtrsim 4$–6. Below threshold: near-neoclassical transport. Above: strongly anomalous.
W7-X stellarator (Germany): no disruptions, steady-state, demonstrated neoclassical transport level 2018.

Thermal Diffusivity
$$\chi_i^{gB} = \frac{\rho_i v_{ti}}{a}\quad\text{(gyro-Bohm)}$$$$\chi_i^{Bohm} = \frac{T}{eB}\quad\text{(Bohm)}$$
Anomalous transport: measured $\chi_i$ is 10–100× neoclassical prediction. ITG (ion temperature gradient) and TEM (trapped electron mode) dominate.
Neoclassical
Banana orbit width $\Delta_b \sim q\rho_i\epsilon^{-1/2}$. Bootstrap current: toroidal current driven by pressure gradient — essential for steady-state tokamak.
ITER confinement projection: $H_{98}\approx1$ (ELMy H-mode). Energy confinement time $\tau_E\approx3.7$ s. ITER relies on gyro-Bohm scaling holding.
