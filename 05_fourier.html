Fourier Transform
$\hat{f}(\xi)=\!\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\!f(x)\,e^{-2\pi i\xi x}\,dx$
Gaussian Self-Duality
$f(x)=e^{-\pi x^2}\;\xrightarrow{\mathcal{F}}\;\hat f(\xi)=e^{-\pi\xi^2}$
Uncertainty principle: $\Delta x\cdot\Delta\xi \geq \tfrac{1}{4\pi}$.
Quantum version: $\Delta x\cdot\Delta p \geq \tfrac{\hbar}{2}$.
Equality holds for the Gaussian — it is the minimum uncertainty state.

Wave Packet Physics
Phase velocity: $v_\phi = \omega/k$
Group velocity: $v_g = d\omega/dk$

The packet envelope travels at $v_g$; the carrier at $v_\phi$. For light in vacuum $v_\phi=v_g=c$.
Quantum Wavepacket
$\psi(x,t)=\int\tilde\psi(k)\,e^{i(kx-\omega t)}\,dk$

Free particle: $\omega=\hbar k^2/2m$ (quadratic dispersion) — the packet spreads.
Dispersion: if $\omega(k)$ is nonlinear, different frequency components travel at different speeds — the packet spreads. This is why optical pulses spread in fiber and why matter waves spread in free space.
