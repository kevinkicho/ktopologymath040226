The Formula
$e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$
$e^{a+i\theta} = e^a(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$
✨ Euler's Identity!
At θ = π:
e^(iπ) + 1 = 0

Often called the most beautiful equation in mathematics.
Why it works
The Taylor series of $e^z$ converges for all $z \in \mathbb{C}$. Expand $e^{i\theta}$, separate real and imaginary parts — you get exactly $\cos\theta$ and $\sin\theta$.

Cauchy's Theorem
If $f$ is holomorphic on and inside $C$, then $\oint_C f(z)\,dz=0$.

Key insight: the value depends only on which singularities are enclosed, not the shape of the path!
Winding Number
$\oint_C \dfrac{dz}{z-a} = 2\pi i\cdot n(C,a)$

where $n(C,a)$ is the winding number of $C$ around $a$.
