The Raychaudhuri equation governs focusing of null geodesic congruences: dθ/dλ = −θ²/2 − σ_μν σ^μν + ω_μν ω^μν − R_μν k^μ k^ν. Under the Null Energy Condition (R_μν k^μ k^ν ≥ 0) and for irrotational congruences (ω=0), θ must decrease and reach −∞ in finite affine parameter — a caustic/singularity.

Trapped Surfaces — A closed 2-surface is trapped when both families of null normals (ingoing and outgoing) have negative expansion θ < 0. Outside the horizon, outgoing light expands (θ>0) and ingoing converges (θ<0). Inside, both converge. Drag the surface radially to watch the outgoing normals flip at the horizon. The existence of a trapped surface is the key hypothesis of Penrose's 1965 theorem.

Hawking Radiation — Near the event horizon, quantum field theory in curved spacetime predicts that virtual particle–antiparticle pairs are promoted to real particles. One partner falls inward (negative energy, reducing M) while the other escapes as thermal radiation at the Hawking temperature T_H = ℏc³/(8πGMk_B). For a solar-mass black hole T_H ≈ 60 nK — colder than the CMB. As M decreases, T_H increases: smaller black holes are hotter and evaporate faster.

Conformal Cyclic Cosmology (CCC) — Roger Penrose proposes that the universe undergoes infinite cycles (aeons). The remote future of each aeon — dominated by massless radiation — is conformally equivalent to the Big Bang of the next. The conformal rescaling Ω maps the future conformal boundary ℐ⁺ of one aeon to the spacelike initial singularity of the next, allowing a smooth crossover. Hawking radiation from supermassive black holes leaves imprints (Penrose's "Hawking points") in the CMB, potentially detectable as anomalous hot spots.
