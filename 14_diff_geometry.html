Cut locus: the set of points where geodesics from $p$ cease to be length-minimizing. On $S^2$, it is the antipodal point — all geodesics (great circles) from $p$ converge there at arc length $\pi R$.
Conjugate points: where the exponential map becomes singular — nearby geodesics in the same direction focus at the same point. On $S^2$, the antipodal point is a conjugate point of multiplicity 1.

Holonomy—
Covariant derivative:
$\nabla_X Y = \partial_X Y + \Gamma^k_{ij}Y^j$

A vector is parallel transported when $\nabla_{\dot\gamma}V=0$ along the curve $\gamma$.

The holonomy (rotation on return) equals the integral of $K\,dA$ over the enclosed region — the Ambrose-Singer theorem.
Space Form
Three space forms:
$S^n$ ($K=+1$): sphere — finite, closed
$\mathbb{R}^n$ ($K=0$): flat — infinite
$\mathbb{H}^n$ ($K=-1$): hyperbolic — infinite, negatively curved

Cosmology: the shape of our universe is determined by its average curvature (Friedmann equations).
