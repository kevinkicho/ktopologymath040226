Gauge Group

$SU(3)_C\times SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y$

The SM gauge group encodes: $SU(3)_C$ — strong color force (8 gluons), $SU(2)_L$ — weak isospin (W bosons, left-handed), $U(1)_Y$ — weak hypercharge (B boson). After SSB: $SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y\to U(1)_{EM}$.

Fermion Content

$Q_L=\begin{pmatrix}u\\d\end{pmatrix}_L,\; u_R,\; d_R$
$L_L=\begin{pmatrix}\nu_e\\e\end{pmatrix}_L,\; e_R$

Left-handed fermions form $SU(2)_L$ doublets; right-handed are singlets. This left-right asymmetry breaks parity P.

Three Generations

Why three copies? Unknown. Each generation heavier than the last. The three families differ only in mass — a deep mystery of the SM.

Color Confinement

Quarks carry color charge (R,G,B). Free color is never observed — only color-neutral (white) hadrons: baryons (RGB) and mesons (color+anticolor). Asymptotic freedom: $\alpha_s\to 0$ at high energy.

Weak CP Violation

The CKM matrix mixing quark generations contains a complex phase, the sole source of CP violation in the SM quark sector.

Feynman Rules

$\mathcal{L}_{QED}=\bar\psi(i\!\not\!\partial-m)\psi-\tfrac{1}{4}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}-e\bar\psi\!\not\!A\psi$

Each diagram term in the S-matrix corresponds to a topology. External lines = particles in/out. Internal lines = propagators. Vertices = coupling constants.

Propagators

$\Delta_F(q)=\dfrac{i}{q^2-m^2+i\epsilon}$
$S_F(p)=\dfrac{i(\not p+m)}{p^2-m^2+i\epsilon}$

Amplitude

$\mathcal{M}\sim\dfrac{g^2}{q^2-m^2}$

For EM: coupling $e$, fine structure $\alpha=e^2/4\pi\approx 1/137$. For QCD: $\alpha_s\approx 0.1$ at $m_Z$. Loops give corrections $\sim\alpha^n$.

Vertices

QED vertex: $-ie\gamma^\mu$

Each QED vertex contributes a factor of $-ie\gamma^\mu$ to the amplitude.

Higgs Potential

$V(\phi)=-\mu^2|\phi|^2+\lambda|\phi|^4$

For $\mu^2>0$: minimum at $|\phi|=v=\sqrt{\mu^2/2\lambda}$, the vacuum expectation value. The field picks a direction — SSB occurs.

Goldstone Theorem

Every spontaneously broken continuous symmetry generates a massless Goldstone boson. $SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y\to U(1)_{EM}$ breaks 3 generators, giving 3 Goldstone bosons.

Higgs Mechanism

In a gauge theory, Goldstone bosons are "eaten" by gauge bosons, becoming their longitudinal polarization — giving them mass. $W^\pm, Z$ become massive; $\gamma$ stays massless.

$m_W=\tfrac{1}{2}gv\approx 80.4\,\text{GeV}$
$m_Z=\tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{g^2+g'^2}\,v\approx 91.2\,\text{GeV}$

Yukawa Coupling

$\mathcal{L}_Y=-y_f\bar\psi_L\phi\psi_R+h.c.$

Fermion masses arise from Yukawa couplings: $m_f=y_f v/\sqrt{2}$. The physical Higgs boson $h$ (125 GeV) is the radial excitation about the minimum.

Grand Unification

The three gauge couplings $\alpha_1,\alpha_2,\alpha_3$ run with energy scale and approximately meet near $10^{15}$ GeV — suggesting a Grand Unified Theory above that scale.

Running Couplings: $\alpha_i(\mu)$ evolve via the renormalization group. $\alpha_3$ (strong) decreases at high energy (asymptotic freedom). All three approximately unify near $10^{15}$ GeV in SUSY extensions.

EW Symmetry Breaking

$V(\phi)=-\mu^2|\phi|^2+\lambda|\phi|^4$

For $\mu^2>0$, $\lambda>0$: the minimum is a circle at $|\phi|=v=\sqrt{\mu^2/2\lambda}$. The field picks one vacuum, breaking $SU(2)_L\times U(1)_Y\to U(1)_{EM}$.

VEV & Boson Masses

$v=\sqrt{\mu^2/2\lambda}\approx 246\,\text{GeV}$
$M_W=\frac{g\,v}{2}\approx 80.4\,\text{GeV}$
$M_Z=\frac{M_W}{\cos\theta_W}\approx 91.2\,\text{GeV}$

The photon $\gamma$ remains massless — $U(1)_{EM}$ is unbroken. $W^\pm$ and $Z$ acquire longitudinal polarizations by "eating" three Goldstone bosons.

Higgs Boson

$m_H=\sqrt{2\mu^2}=\sqrt{4\lambda}\,v\approx 125\,\text{GeV}$

The physical Higgs is radial oscillations about $|\phi|=v$. Discovered at LHC in 2012. Its mass fixes $\lambda\approx 0.13$.

Weak Mixing Angle

$\sin^2\theta_W=1-(M_W/M_Z)^2\approx 0.231$

$\theta_W$ (Weinberg angle) mixes $W^3_\mu$ and $B_\mu$ to produce the physical $Z$ and $\gamma$. Measured at LEP to extraordinary precision.

Phase Transition

At temperature $T\sim 159$ GeV in the early universe, the EW phase transition occurred: the Higgs field acquired its VEV, giving mass to W and Z. This is when baryogenesis constraints apply.

CKM Matrix

$V_{CKM}=\begin{pmatrix}V_{ud}&V_{us}&V_{ub}\\V_{cd}&V_{cs}&V_{cb}\\V_{td}&V_{ts}&V_{tb}\end{pmatrix}$

Mixes quark mass eigenstates with weak eigenstates. Unitarity: $V^\dagger V=1$. Contains one irreducible CP-violating phase $\delta$.

Wolfenstein Parametrization

$\lambda=\sin\theta_C\approx 0.225$
$V_{us}\approx\lambda,\; V_{cb}\approx A\lambda^2$
$V_{ub}\approx A\lambda^3(\bar\rho-i\bar\eta)$

Four real parameters: $\lambda, A, \bar\rho, \bar\eta$. CP violation requires $\bar\eta\neq 0$.

Unitarity Triangle

$V_{ud}V_{ub}^*+V_{cd}V_{cb}^*+V_{td}V_{tb}^*=0$

Dividing by $V_{cd}V_{cb}^*$: a triangle in the complex $(\bar\rho, \bar\eta)$ plane. Its area $= J/2$ (Jarlskog invariant $J\approx 3.3\times10^{-5}$).

CP Violation

$J=\text{Im}(V_{us}V_{cb}V_{ub}^*V_{cs}^*)$

Non-zero triangle area signals CP violation. Measured in $B$ factories (BaBar, Belle) and LHCb via $B^0\to J/\psi K_S$, $B\to\pi\pi$ etc. The SM has too little CP violation for baryogenesis — new physics needed.

Angles α, β, γ

$\beta=\arg\!\left(-\frac{V_{cd}V_{cb}^*}{V_{td}V_{tb}^*}\right)\approx 21°$

$\sin 2\beta$ is golden measurement at $B$ factories. All constraints must be consistent with one triangle — SM consistency test.

Renormalization Group

$\mu\frac{d\alpha_i}{d\mu}=\frac{b_i\,\alpha_i^2}{2\pi}$

One-loop RGE: couplings "run" logarithmically with energy scale $\mu$. The $b_i$ coefficients encode the particle content of the theory.

SM β-function Coefficients

$b_1=+\tfrac{41}{10},\; b_2=-\tfrac{19}{6},\; b_3=-7$

$\alpha_3$ (strong) decreases at high energy: asymptotic freedom ($b_3<0$). $\alpha_1$ (hypercharge) increases. In SM, the three lines do NOT meet at one point.

MSSM Coefficients

$b_1=+\tfrac{33}{5},\; b_2=+1,\; b_3=-3$

Adding SUSY partners shifts the $\beta$-functions. The three couplings converge near $M_{GUT}\approx 2\times10^{16}$ GeV — striking hint for SUSY GUT.

Grand Unification

At $M_{GUT}$, all three forces merge into a single gauge group, e.g. $SU(5)$ (Georgi–Glashow) or $SO(10)$. Predicts proton decay (not yet observed). The MSSM unification is one of the strongest indirect arguments for SUSY.

$\alpha_3^{-1}(m_Z)\approx8.5$

Evolved from precision measurements at the $Z$ pole (LEP, SLD).

PMNS Matrix

$U_{PMNS}=R_{23}(\theta_{23})\cdot R_{13}(\theta_{13},\delta_{CP})\cdot R_{12}(\theta_{12})$

Analogous to CKM for leptons. Mixes neutrino flavor eigenstates ($\nu_e,\nu_\mu,\nu_\tau$) with mass eigenstates ($\nu_1,\nu_2,\nu_3$).

Oscillation Formula

$P(\nu_\alpha\to\nu_\beta)=\left|\sum_i U_{\alpha i}^*U_{\beta i}\,e^{-im_i^2 L/2E}\right|^2$

Two-flavor approximation: $P(\nu_\mu\to\nu_e)\approx\sin^2(2\theta)\sin^2\!\left(\frac{\Delta m^2 L}{4E}\right)$

Experimental Values

$\theta_{12}\approx33°,\;\theta_{23}\approx45°,\;\theta_{13}\approx8.5°$
$\Delta m^2_{21}\approx7.5\times10^{-5}\,\text{eV}^2$
$|\Delta m^2_{31}|\approx2.5\times10^{-3}\,\text{eV}^2$

Large mixing angles — very different from the CKM matrix (small quark mixing). Measured by SNO, Super-K, KamLAND, Daya Bay, T2K, NOvA.

MSW Matter Effect

Mikheyev–Smirnov–Wolfenstein effect: coherent forward scattering of $\nu_e$ on electrons in matter adds an effective potential, shifting the mixing. Explains solar neutrino deficit (matter-enhanced oscillation).

$A_{MSW}=\sqrt{2}\,G_F\,N_e$

Mass Hierarchy

Normal hierarchy: $m_1<m_2<m_3$. Inverted: $m_3<m_1<m_2$. Absolute mass scale unknown ($\sum m_\nu<0.12$ eV from cosmology). CP violation in neutrino sector ($\delta_{CP}$) could explain matter–antimatter asymmetry (leptogenesis).

Gauge Anomalies

Quantum corrections can destroy the gauge invariance needed for renormalizability. Triangle Feynman diagrams with three gauge currents at the vertices generate anomalies proportional to traces over fermion representations.

Anomaly Conditions

$\text{U(1)}^3:\;\sum_f Y_f^3=0$
$\text{SU(2)}^2\text{U(1)}:\;\sum_{\text{doublets}}Y=0$
$\text{SU(3)}^2\text{U(1)}:\;\sum_{\text{triplets}}Y=0$
$\text{Grav}^2\text{U(1)}:\;\sum_f Y_f=0$

All four conditions must hold simultaneously for the SM to be anomaly-free and self-consistent.

Quarks vs Leptons

Quarks come in 3 colors, contributing a factor of 3. This exact color multiplicity is required for anomaly cancellation — a profound link between the strong and electroweak sectors.

B−L Anomaly

Adding a right-handed neutrino $\nu_R$ (singlet under $SU(2)_L\times SU(3)_C$, $Y=0$) completes $B-L$ anomaly cancellation, allowing a gauged $U(1)_{B-L}$ extension. This motivates seesaw mechanism for neutrino masses.

Seesaw Mechanism

$m_\nu\approx\frac{y_\nu^2 v^2}{M_R}$

Heavy Majorana mass $M_R\sim10^{14}$ GeV for $\nu_R$ explains tiny neutrino masses via seesaw. Leptogenesis: CP-violating decays of $\nu_R$ in the early universe generate baryon asymmetry.
