$\Sigma(p)\!=\!\int\!\frac{d^4k}{(2\pi)^4}\frac{1}{(k^2\!-\!m^2)((k\!+\!p)^2\!-\!m^2)}$

Fock Space & Second Quantization

Occupation number basis:
$|n_1,n_2,n_3,\ldots\rangle$

Bosons: $n_k \in \{0,1,2,\ldots\}$
Fermions: $n_k \in \{0,1\}$ (Pauli)

$\hat{a}^\dagger_k|n_k\rangle = \sqrt{n_k+1}\,|n_k+1\rangle$
$\hat{a}_k|n_k\rangle = \sqrt{n_k}\,|n_k-1\rangle$
Commutation relations:
Bosons: $[\hat{a}_k,\hat{a}^\dagger_{k'}] = \delta_{kk'}$
Fermions: $\{\hat{c}_k,\hat{c}^\dagger_{k'}\} = \delta_{kk'}$

Vacuum state:
$\hat{a}_k|0\rangle = 0 \;\forall k$

$|n_{k_1}\rangle = \frac{(\hat{a}^\dagger_{k_1})^{n}}{\sqrt{n!}}|0\rangle$
Field operator:
$\hat\phi(x) = \int\!\frac{d^3k}{(2\pi)^3}\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\omega_k}}\!\left(\hat{a}_k e^{ikx}+\hat{a}^\dagger_k e^{-ikx}\right)$

Each Fourier mode is a quantum harmonic oscillator. The field is an infinite collection of oscillators.
Pauli exclusion from anticommutators:

RG Flow & Fixed Points

β-function:
$\beta(g) = \mu\dfrac{dg}{d\mu}$

Flow arrows show $-\nabla\beta$ in coupling space $(g_1,g_2)$.
Wilson-Fisher fixed point (φ⁴, d=3):
$g^* \approx \frac{16\pi^2\epsilon}{3}$ where $\epsilon=4-d$

In $d=3$: $\epsilon=1$, gives Ising universality class.
Controls 2nd-order phase transitions, critical exponents $\nu,\eta$.
Operator classification:
Relevant: $\Delta < d$, grows in IR
Marginal: $\Delta = d$, log running
Irrelevant: $\Delta > d$, shrinks in IR

Near fixed point $g^*$: $g-g^* \sim \mu^{-y_i}$
$y_i = d-\Delta_i$ is the RG eigenvalue.
UV vs IR attractors:
UV fixed point: flow runs toward it as $\mu\to\infty$ (asymptotic freedom)
IR fixed point: flow runs toward it as $\mu\to 0$ (conformal IR)

LSZ Reduction & S-matrix

LSZ formula:
$\langle f|S|i\rangle = \prod_j \left(\frac{i}{p_j^2-m^2}\right)\cdot\widetilde{G}^{(n)}(p_1,\ldots)$

Amputate external propagators and go on-shell.
Steps:
1. Compute full n-point Green's function
   $G^{(n)}(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = \langle 0|T\{\hat\phi(x_1)\cdots\hat\phi(x_n)\}|0\rangle$

2. Fourier transform to momentum space

3. Identify on-shell poles $p^2\to m^2$ in each leg

4. Multiply by $(p^2-m^2)/iZ^{1/2}$ per external leg

5. The residue is $\langle f|S|i\rangle$ (the amplitude $\mathcal{M}$)
On-shell pole:
$\tilde\Delta(p) = \frac{i}{p^2-m^2+i\epsilon}\xrightarrow{p^2\to m^2}\frac{iZ}{p^2-m^2}$

The residue $Z$ is the field-strength renormalization.
2→2 scattering:
$|\mathcal{M}|^2$ gives the differential cross section
$\frac{d\sigma}{d\Omega} = \frac{|\mathcal{M}|^2}{64\pi^2 s}$

Mandelstam variables: $s=(p_1+p_2)^2$, $t=(p_1-p_3)^2$, $u=(p_1-p_4)^2$, $s+t+u=4m^2$.

Coleman-Weinberg Effective Potential

Effective potential:
$V_{\rm eff}(\phi) = V_{\rm cl} + \hbar\,V_{1\text{-loop}}$

$V_{\rm cl} = \tfrac{1}{2}\mu^2\phi^2 + \tfrac{\lambda}{4!}\phi^4$

$V_{1\text{-loop}} = \frac{1}{64\pi^2}m^4(\phi)\!\left[\ln\frac{m^2(\phi)}{\Lambda^2}-\tfrac{3}{2}\right]$
where $m^2(\phi)=\mu^2+\tfrac{\lambda}{2}\phi^2$
Radiative symmetry breaking:
Even if $\mu^2>0$ classically (no SSB), quantum corrections can generate a nontrivial minimum at $\phi\ne 0$.

This is the Coleman-Weinberg mechanism (1973). Applied to scalar QED, spontaneous gauge symmetry breaking occurs radiatively.
Running minimum:
As $\mu$ (RG scale) runs, the minimum $\phi_{\min}$ shifts. The shape of $V_{\rm eff}$ changes — shown by the animated curve.

Anomalies & Anomaly Cancellation

Chiral anomaly:
$\partial_\mu j^{5\mu} = \frac{e^2}{16\pi^2}F_{\mu\nu}\tilde F^{\mu\nu}$

The axial current $j^5_\mu = \bar\psi\gamma_\mu\gamma_5\psi$ is classically conserved but broken at 1-loop by a triangle diagram (Fujikawa).
Triangle diagram (AVV):
One axial vertex (A) and two vector vertices (V). The loop integral is ambiguous; regularization breaks axial symmetry.

The anomaly coefficient:
$\mathcal{A} = \sum_f Q_f^2 \cdot \text{tr}(T_A)$

Must cancel for consistency: SM fermion content is carefully chosen so $\sum_f Q_f^3 = 0$ etc.
π⁰→γγ:
$\Gamma(\pi^0\!\to\!\gamma\gamma) = \frac{\alpha^2 m_\pi^3}{64\pi^3 f_\pi^2}\cdot N_c^2 \left(\sum Q^2\right)^2$

Experiment: $\tau\approx 8.3\times10^{-17}$ s
Theory with $N_c=3$: matches to 1%! This is one of the triumphs of the anomaly calculation.
't Hooft anomaly matching:
Anomalies of global symmetries are RG invariant. They must match between UV and IR descriptions of the same theory.

Used to constrain IR phases of strongly coupled theories (confinement, chiral symmetry breaking).
