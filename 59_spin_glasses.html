Edwards-Anderson Model (1975)
Hamiltonian with quenched random bonds:
H = −Σ J_ij S_i S_j
J_ij drawn i.i.d. from ±J with probability p (AFM) or (1−p) (FM). Quenched disorder: bonds fixed, spins thermalize.
Frustration
A plaquette (elementary square loop) is frustrated when the product of its four bond signs is negative: Π J_ij < 0. No spin configuration can satisfy all bonds simultaneously — the system is "frustrated".

EA Order Parameter
q_EA = [⟨S_i⟩²]_av — time average then disorder average. Nonzero below T_g signals spin glass order without net magnetization. Specific heat shows broad cusp (no latent heat — not first-order).
Experimental Realizations
CuMn, AuFe dilute alloys: magnetic Mn/Fe impurities in nonmagnetic host → RKKY interactions with random sign. T_g depends on impurity concentration. de Almeida-Thouless (AT) line: critical field H_AT ∝ (T_g−T)^(3/2) above which spin glass order is destroyed by field.

The Replica Trick
log Z = lim_{n→0} (Z^n − 1)/n
Compute [Z^n]_disorder for integer n, then analytically continue n→0. Replicas a=1..n are n identical copies of the system in the same disorder. Their mutual overlap Q_ab = (1/N)Σ_i S_i^a S_i^b encodes correlations.
Replica Symmetry Breaking
Replica-symmetric (RS) ansatz: Q_ab = q for all a≠b. Works above T_g; fails below — negative entropy catastrophe (Sherrington-Kirkpatrick 1975). Parisi 1979-83: hierarchical RSB — Q_ab takes values at k levels, with q(x) becoming continuous (full RSB) as k→∞.
Parisi q(x) Function
q(x) for x∈[0,1] is the order parameter function. Physical meaning: the probability that two randomly chosen equilibrium states have overlap ≤ q is x = P(q). The ultrametric tree structure means state distances satisfy d(A,C) ≤ max(d(A,B),d(B,C)).
Nobel Prize 2021
Giorgio Parisi received the Nobel Prize in Physics 2021 "for the discovery of the interplay of disorder and fluctuations in physical systems from atomic to planetary scales." The Parisi formula for the SK free energy was proven rigorously by Guerra-Toninelli (2003) and Talagrand (2006).

Hopfield Network (1982)
N Ising neurons S_i ∈ {±1}. Store p patterns ξ^μ via Hebbian weights:
W_ij = (1/N) Σ_μ ξ_i^μ ξ_j^μ
Energy: E = −½ Σ_{ij} W_ij S_i S_j. Stored patterns are energy minima (attractors). Retrieval = energy minimization.
Storage Capacity
α_c = p/N ≈ 0.138 (Amit-Gutfreund-Sompolinsky 1985). Below α_c: clean retrieval. Above α_c: spurious states (mixtures of patterns), retrieval errors. The Hopfield network IS an SK spin glass with structured couplings.
Modern Hopfield Networks
Ramsauer et al. (2020): modern Hopfield networks with continuous states and exponential capacity ~ exp(N/2). Key insight: transformer self-attention = Hopfield retrieval step. Softmax attention = soft version of winner-take-all retrieval dynamics.
Connection to SK Model
SK model: J_ij fully random (Gaussian). Hopfield: J_ij = low-rank structured random (rank-p outer products). Below capacity: effective paramagnetic behavior in pattern space. Above: spin glass-like frozen disorder with spurious attractors.

Simulated Annealing (1983)
Kirkpatrick, Gelatt & Vecchi (Science 1983, ~35,000 citations): use Metropolis MC at decreasing T to solve combinatorial optimization. The spin glass energy landscape is isomorphic to the TSP cost landscape — exponentially many local minima separated by barriers.
TSP as a Spin Glass
Traveling Salesman Problem: find shortest tour through N cities. NP-hard in general (Cook 1971). The partition function Z(β) = Σ_tours exp(−βL) matches a random disordered system's partition function. Slow annealing finds near-optimal solutions — the physics insight that earned this method its power.
Quantum Annealing
Replace thermal fluctuations with quantum tunneling: H = −Γ Σ σ_i^x − Σ J_ij σ_i^z σ_j^z. Transverse field Γ→0 slowly (adiabatic). D-Wave systems implement ~5000-qubit quantum annealing. QAOA (Quantum Approximate Optimization Algorithm) encodes combinatorial problems in quantum circuits.
Annealing Schedules
Exponential: T(t) = T_0 · α^t (fast, can miss global minimum). Linear: T(t) = T_0(1 − t/t_max) (simple, often too fast). Logarithmic: T(t) = c/log(1+t) (guaranteed convergence to global min, impractically slow). Adaptive schedules track acceptance rate.

SK Model (1975)
Sherrington-Kirkpatrick: N spins, all-to-all couplings J_ij ~ N(0, J/√N). Exactly solvable in the thermodynamic limit via replica method. TAP equations (Thouless-Anderson-Palmer 1977): m_i = tanh(β Σ_j J_ij m_j − β²(1−q)m_i) — the last term is the Onsager reaction field correcting naive MF.
Cavity Method
Mézard-Parisi cavity (1987): compute partition function by adding one spin at a time. Belief propagation = cavity equations on tree-like graphs. Message passing algorithms (LDPC decoding, compressed sensing, random K-SAT) all follow from cavity method — spin glass physics → information theory.
Protein Folding & Biology
Bryngelson-Wolynes (1987): protein folding energy landscape as a random heteropolymer spin glass. Funnel landscape: native state is the global minimum below the glass transition temperature T_f. Frustration between hydrophobic collapse and secondary structure — explains Levinthal's paradox of fast folding.
Finance & Random Matrices
Markowitz portfolio optimization → correlated returns matrix → spin glass in external field. Random matrix theory (Marchenko-Pastur law) distinguishes signal from noise in asset correlations. Spin glass transition in portfolio optimization: below critical temperature, optimal portfolio concentrates — "freezing" of capital allocation.
