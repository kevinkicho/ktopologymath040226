Time Control
Spreading packet: the ionized electron's wavefunction disperses in ~10 fs. Attosecond pulses capture the motion before significant spreading occurs.

HHG Physics
$$U_p = \frac{e^2 E_0^2}{4m_e\omega^2} \approx 9.33\,I_{14}\lambda_\mu^2\;\text{eV}$$
$$E_{cut} = I_p + 3.17\,U_p$$
Phase matching: $\Delta k = k_q - q\,k_L \approx 0$
Three-step model (Corkum 1993, Kulander 1993):
1. Tunnel ionization — strong field suppresses Coulomb barrier; electron tunnels out near peak field.
2. Free acceleration — electron quivers in laser field, gaining ponderomotive energy $U_p$.
3. Recollision — when field reverses, electron returns and emits XUV photon up to $I_p+3.17U_p$.
Plateau: harmonics of equal intensity up to cutoff; then exponential rolloff. Only odd harmonics for linearly polarized light (centro-symmetric medium).
Quasi-phase-matching in modulated waveguide selects short or long electron trajectories, enabling isolated attosecond pulse (IAP) generation with few-cycle pulses.

Eisenbud-Wigner-Smith (EWS) delay: derivative of the scattering phase shift with respect to energy. Different partial waves ($\ell=0,1,2$…) acquire different delays — measured with attosecond resolution.
Key experiments:
• Schultze et al., Science 328, 1658 (2010) — Ne 2s vs 2p: ~21 as
• Klünder et al., PRL 106, 143002 (2011) — Ar RABBITT
• Isinger et al., Science 358, 893 (2017) — angular EWS
• Wigner, Phys. Rev. 98, 145 (1955) — original theory
Tunneling time controversy: Salières-Maquet debate; attoclock suggests near-instantaneous or imaginary tunneling time — still open question.

Ghimire 2011: First observation of solid-state HHG from ZnO crystal. Even harmonics appear due to broken inversion symmetry — band structure is imprinted in the harmonic spectrum.
ATAS (Attosecond Transient Absorption) probes core-level transitions with sub-femtosecond resolution in solids.
Solid-State HHG
$$j(t) = \frac{e\hbar}{m}\sum_k \nabla_k\varepsilon_k\,f_k(t)$$
intraband current
Berry curvature: $\Omega_k = \nabla_k \times \langle u_k|\nabla_k|u_k\rangle$
Two mechanisms:
• Intraband: electron wavepacket accelerated in band, nonlinear current from band curvature.
• Interband: Zener tunneling across band gap → recombination → photon emission (analogous to gas HHG).
Ultrafast demagnetization: Beaurepaire 1996 showed 300 fs demagnetization; attosecond science aims to resolve sub-fs spin dynamics via ultrafast MOKE (magneto-optical Kerr effect).
2D materials: WSe₂ and graphene host Berry curvature-driven anomalous Hall currents measurable with attosecond pulses. Prospect: attosecond electron microscopy at sub-Å resolution.

FEL Physics
$$\lambda_r = \frac{\lambda_u}{2\gamma^2}\left(1+\frac{K^2}{2}\right)$$
resonance condition, $K=eB_0\lambda_u/2\pi m_ec$
Pierce parameter: $\rho \sim \left(\frac{I/I_A}{16\gamma^3}\frac{K^2[JJ]^2}{(1+K^2/2)^2\sigma_\perp^2 k_u^2}\right)^{1/3}$
Diffract before destroy (Neutze 2000): ultrashort XFEL pulse illuminates single protein molecule; scattered photons recorded before Coulomb explosion. Goal: atomic-resolution structure without crystals.
Femtosecond crystallography: Chapman et al. 2011 demonstrated serial femtosecond crystallography (SFX) with membrane proteins. Enables room-temperature structures and time-resolved snapshots.
Toward attosecond XFELs: Seeded XFELs (HGHG, EEHG) produce transform-limited pulses; planned attosecond XFEL facilities aim for <100 as X-ray pulses for core-level electron dynamics.
