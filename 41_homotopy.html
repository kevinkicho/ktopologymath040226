π₁ — Fundamental Group

  • $\pi_1(S^1)=\mathbb{Z}$ — loops wind around a circle
  • $\pi_1(S^2)=0$ — sphere is simply connected
  • $\pi_1(T^2)=\mathbb{Z}^2$ — torus has two generators
  • $\pi_1(\mathbb{R}^2\setminus\{p\})=\mathbb{Z}$ — punctured plane
  • $\pi_1(F_2)$ — figure-8 gives free group
Van Kampen's theorem: If $X=A\cup B$ with $A\cap B$ path-connected, then $\pi_1(X)\cong\pi_1(A)*_{\pi_1(A\cap B)}\pi_1(B)$. This allows computing $\pi_1$ by cutting a space into simpler pieces.
Homotopy equivalence: $f,g:X\to Y$ are homotopic if there is a continuous $H:X\times[0,1]\to Y$ with $H(\cdot,0)=f$ and $H(\cdot,1)=g$. Spaces with the same homotopy type share all $\pi_n$.
Deformation retract: A subspace $A\subset X$ is a deformation retract if there is a homotopy $H:X\times I\to X$ with $H(x,0)=x$, $H(x,1)\in A$, $H(a,t)=a$ for $a\in A$.
Orange loops wind around a hole: $[\gamma]\neq e\in\pi_1$. Blue loops are contractible: $[\gamma]=e$.

Universal cover of $S^1$: the real line $\mathbb{R}$, with covering map $p(t)=e^{2\pi it}$. Going around $S^1$ once lifts to moving by 1 on $\mathbb{R}$.

Covering Spaces

  • $p:\tilde{X}\to X$ — covering map, every $x$ has evenly covered nbd
  • $p:\mathbb{R}\to S^1$ — $p(t)=e^{2\pi it}$, fiber $= \mathbb{Z}$
  • $\pi_1(S^1)\cong\mathbb{Z}$ — deck group of universal cover
  • Monodromy: $\pi_1(B)\to\mathrm{Sym}(F)$
  • Galois correspondence — subgroups $\leftrightarrow$ covers
Unique path lifting: Given a path $\gamma$ in the base $B$ starting at $b_0$ and a lift $\tilde{b}_0\in p^{-1}(b_0)$, there is a unique lift $\tilde\gamma$ in $\tilde X$ starting at $\tilde{b}_0$.
Deck transformations are self-homeomorphisms $\tilde X\to\tilde X$ commuting with $p$. For the universal cover they form a group isomorphic to $\pi_1(X)$.
Figure-8 universal cover: an infinite tree — the Cayley graph of the free group $F_2=\langle a,b\rangle$. Each vertex has 4 edges labeled $a,b,a^{-1},b^{-1}$.

Theory

  • Knot group $= \pi_1(S^3\setminus K)$
  • Skein: $t^{-1}V_+ - tV_- = (t^{1/2}-t^{-1/2})V_0$
  • Seifert surface — orientable surface with $\partial = K$
  • CS theory $Z=\int\mathcal{D}A\,e^{ik\,\text{CS}(A)}$
Chern-Simons / Witten: The Jones polynomial arises from Chern-Simons gauge theory with gauge group $SU(2)$. The partition function $Z(S^3,K)$ evaluated in this TQFT gives knot invariants — a profound link between physics and topology.
Reidemeister moves I, II, III generate all isotopies of knot diagrams. Two diagrams represent the same knot iff connected by a finite sequence of these moves.

Higher Homotopy Groups

  • $\pi_n(S^n)=\mathbb{Z}$ — degree of maps $S^n\to S^n$
  • $\pi_1(S^2)=0$ — sphere is simply connected
  • $\pi_2(S^2)=\mathbb{Z}$ — Hopf's theorem (1931)
  • $\pi_3(S^2)=\mathbb{Z}$ — Hopf fibration $\eta$
  • $\pi_3(S^3)=\mathbb{Z}$ — identity map generator
  • $\pi_4(S^3)=\mathbb{Z}/2$ — first non-trivial unstable
Hopf fibration: $h:S^3\to S^2$. Fibres are great circles $S^1\subset S^3$, any two fibres are linked. This is the generator $\eta$ of $\pi_3(S^2)\cong\mathbb{Z}$, discovered by Hopf in 1931.
Long exact sequence of a fibration $F\hookrightarrow E\to B$:
$\cdots\to\pi_n(F)\to\pi_n(E)\to\pi_n(B)\to\pi_{n-1}(F)\to\cdots$
For Hopf: $S^1\to S^3\to S^2$ gives $\pi_n(S^3)\cong\pi_n(S^2)$ for $n\geq 3$.
Degree: For $f:S^n\to S^n$, $\deg(f)=$ number of preimages of a regular value (with sign). $\deg(\mathrm{id})=1$, $\deg(\mathrm{const})=0$, $\deg(\mathrm{antipodal})=(-1)^{n+1}$.

Torus $T^2$ built from 1 vertex, 2 edges ($a,b$), 1 face. Attaching map: $aba^{-1}b^{-1}$. By van Kampen: $\pi_1(T^2)\cong\mathbb{Z}^2$.

CW Complexes & Retracts

  • Torus: 1 point + 2 loops + 1 square
  • $S^n$: 1 zero-cell + 1 $n$-cell
  • $\mathbb{R}P^2$: one cell in each dimension 0–2
  • Euler char: $\chi=V-E+F$ (cells)
  • Torus: $\chi=1-2+1=0$ ✓
  • $S^2$: $\chi=1+0+1=2$ ✓
Deformation retract: The Möbius band $M$ deformation retracts onto its central circle $S^1$: move every point radially toward the core. Thus $\pi_1(M)\cong\mathbb{Z}$.
$\mathbb{R}^n\setminus\{0\}\simeq S^{n-1}$: The retraction $r(x)=x/|x|$ gives a deformation retraction via $H(x,t)=x/|x|^t$. So $\pi_k(\mathbb{R}^n\setminus\{0\})\cong\pi_k(S^{n-1})$.
Attaching maps determine the topology. The torus word $aba^{-1}b^{-1}$ gives abelian $\pi_1$; the Klein bottle $abab^{-1}$ gives non-abelian $\pi_1$.

Vortex in superfluid: order parameter $\psi=|\psi|e^{i\theta}$ winds $n$ times around the core. Classified by $\pi_1(S^1)=\mathbb{Z}$, winding number $n$.

Topological Defects

  • $\pi_1(S^1)=\mathbb{Z}$ → vortex lines (superfluids, nematics)
  • $\pi_1(SO(3))=\mathbb{Z}/2$ → disclinations in crystals
  • $\pi_2(S^2)=\mathbb{Z}$ → monopoles (hedgehog)
  • $\pi_3(S^3)=\mathbb{Z}$ → Skyrmions (baryons!)
  • $\pi_3(S^2)=\mathbb{Z}$ → Hopf solitons in nematics
't Hooft–Polyakov monopole: A hedgehog $\hat n=\hat r$ at spatial infinity defines a map $S^2\to S^2$ of degree 1. The magnetic charge is quantized: $g=4\pi n/e$ (Dirac condition).
Skyrmion: In nuclear physics (Skyrme 1961), baryons are topological solitons: maps $S^3\to SU(2)\cong S^3$ with degree = baryon number. Topological stability prevents decay.
Vortex quantization: In a BEC/superconductor, circulation $\oint\mathbf{v}\cdot d\mathbf{l}=nh/m$. The winding number $n\in\mathbb{Z}$ is a topological invariant — cannot change without creating/annihilating defects.

Bott periodicity: $\pi_{n+2}(U(\infty))\cong\pi_n(U(\infty))$. Period 2 for unitary groups: $0, \mathbb{Z}, 0, \mathbb{Z}, \ldots$

Bott Periodicity & K-theory

  • $\pi_0(U)=0$ (connected)
  • $\pi_1(U)=\mathbb{Z}$ (winding number)
  • $\pi_2(U)=0$
  • $\pi_3(U)=\mathbb{Z}$ (Skyrmion charge)
  • Period 2: $\pi_{n+2}\cong\pi_n$
Orthogonal period 8: $\pi_*(O(\infty))$ cycles through $\mathbb{Z}/2,\mathbb{Z}/2,0,\mathbb{Z},0,0,0,\mathbb{Z}$ then repeats. This is the famous "8-fold way" in topology.
K-theory: $\tilde{K}(S^n)\cong\pi_{n-1}(U(\infty))$ by Bott periodicity. So $\tilde{K}(S^{\mathrm{even}})\cong\mathbb{Z}$, $\tilde{K}(S^{\mathrm{odd}})=0$ (complex K-theory).
10 symmetry classes: Combining time-reversal $T$, particle-hole $C$, and chiral $S$ symmetries gives 10 Altland-Zirnbauer classes. In each spatial dimension $d$, topological phases are classified by $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathbb{Z}/2$, or $0$ — the "periodic table of topological matter."
