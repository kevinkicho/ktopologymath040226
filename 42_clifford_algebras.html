Clifford Algebra $\text{Cl}(p,q)$

$\text{Cl}(p,q)$ has $2^{p+q}$ basis elements generated by $\{e_1,\ldots,e_{p+q}\}$ with
$e_i e_j + e_j e_i = 2\eta_{ij}$
where $\eta_{ij}=+1$ for $i\le p$, $-1$ for $i>p$.
Select a node to see its multiplication table and basis blades.
Grade structure — $\text{Cl}(3,0)$:
Grade 0: 1 (scalar) Grade 1: e₁ e₂ e₃ (vectors) Grade 2: e₁₂ e₁₃ e₂₃ (bivectors) Grade 3: e₁₂₃ (pseudoscalar)
Periodicity Theorem:
$\text{Cl}(p{+}8,q)\cong\text{Cl}(p,q)\otimes\text{Mat}(16,\mathbb{R})$
The pattern of Clifford algebras repeats with period 8 (Bott periodicity). This underlies K-theory and the classification of topological insulators.
Spin Groups:
$\text{Spin}(n)\subset\text{Cl}(n,0)$ — the double cover of $SO(n)$. Spinors are elements of the minimal left ideal of $\text{Cl}(n,0)$.

Geometric Algebra $\text{Cl}(2,0)$

Basis: $\{1,\,e_1,\,e_2,\,e_{12}\}$ where $e_{12}^2=-1$.
The pseudoscalar $I=e_{12}$ acts as the imaginary unit in 2D!
Geometric Product:
$ab = a\cdot b + a\wedge b$

Inner product: $a\cdot b = \tfrac{1}{2}(ab+ba)$
Outer product: $a\wedge b = \tfrac{1}{2}(ab-ba)$

Together these form a multivector — a scalar plus a bivector.
Rotor & Rotation:
$R = e^{e_{12}\theta/2} = \cos\tfrac{\theta}{2} + \sin\tfrac{\theta}{2}\,e_{12}$

Rotate $v$: $v' = RvR^\dagger$
where $R^\dagger$ reverses the blade order.
Double Cover $\text{Spin}(n)\to SO(n)$:
Both $R$ and $-R$ give the same rotation. A spinor requires a 720° rotation to return to itself — the hallmark of spin-½ particles.

Spacetime Algebra $\text{Cl}(1,3)$

16 basis blades grouped by grade:
0: $1$
1: $\gamma^0,\gamma^1,\gamma^2,\gamma^3$ (spacetime vectors)
2: $\gamma^{01},\gamma^{02},\ldots,\gamma^{23}$ (EM tensor!)
3: $\gamma^{012},\ldots,\gamma^{123}$
4: $\gamma^{0123}=I$ (pseudoscalar)
Electromagnetic Field:
$F = \mathbf{E} + I\mathbf{B}$
Both $\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ encoded in ONE geometric object — a bivector in $\text{Cl}(1,3)$!
Dirac Equation (GA form):
$\nabla\psi\, I\sigma_3 = m\psi\gamma^0$
A single geometric equation replacing 4 coupled complex PDEs.
Spinor as Flag:
A spinor = flagpole (vector) + flag (half-plane). Rotating 360° returns the flag upside-down (sign change). 720° restores it — the spin-½ phenomenon.
Rotation of spinors:
$\psi \to e^{I\theta/2}\,\psi$
Pauli matrices $= $ basis of $\text{Cl}_{\text{even}}(3)\cong\mathbb{H}$.
Dirac spinor $=$ element of minimal left ideal of $\text{Cl}(1,3)$.

Defining relation:
$e_i e_j + e_j e_i = 2\delta_{ij}$
(all generators square to $+1$)
Graded structure:
Grade 0: 1 Grade 1: e₁ e₂ e₃ Grade 2: e₁₂ e₁₃ e₂₃ Grade 3: e₁₂₃

Isomorphism:
$\text{Cl}(3,0)\cong\text{Mat}(2,\mathbb{C})$
The even sub-algebra $\text{Cl}^+(3,0)\cong\mathbb{H}$ (quaternions), with $e_{12},e_{13},e_{23}$ mapping to $i,j,k$.
Anticommutativity:
$e_i e_j = -e_j e_i$ for $i\ne j$
$e_i^2 = +1$ for all $i$
$(e_{12})^2 = e_1 e_2 e_1 e_2 = -e_1 e_1 e_2 e_2 = -1$

Rotors in $\text{Cl}(3,0)$

Rotor in the $e_1 e_2$ plane:
$R = \cos\tfrac{\theta}{2} + \sin\tfrac{\theta}{2}\, e_{12}$

Rotates vector $v$:
$v' = R v R^\dagger$
where $R^\dagger = \cos\tfrac{\theta}{2} - \sin\tfrac{\theta}{2}\, e_{12}$
Double cover $\text{Spin}(3)\to SO(3)$:
Both $R$ and $-R$ produce identical rotations: $(-R)v(-R^\dagger) = RvR^\dagger$.

Full $2\pi$ rotation: $R\to -R$ (sign flip!)
Full $4\pi$ rotation: $R\to R$ (restored)
Spinor behavior:
A spinor $\psi$ transforms as $\psi\to R\psi$. Under $\theta\to\theta+2\pi$: $\psi\to -\psi$. This is the mathematical origin of spin-½ in quantum mechanics.
Dirac Belt Trick:
A belt attached at both ends: one rotation by $2\pi$ tangles it, but $4\pi$ unentangles — demonstrating $\pi_1(SO(3))=\mathbb{Z}_2$ and its double cover $\text{Spin}(3)\cong SU(2)\cong S^3$.

1x Spinors live in the minimal left ideal $S = \text{Cl}(3,0)\cdot f$

Spinors as Left Ideals

Primitive idempotent:
$f = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 + e_3)$
$f^2 = f$, $\quad f\ne 0,\,f\ne 1$

The minimal left ideal:
$S = \text{Cl}(3,0)\cdot f$
Spinor basis:
$S$ has basis $\{f,\, e_1 f\}$:
$f = \tfrac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix}+\tfrac{1}{2}\begin{pmatrix}0\\0\end{pmatrix}$... actually:
$f\leftrightarrow\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix}$, $e_1 f\leftrightarrow\begin{pmatrix}0\\1\end{pmatrix}$
Pauli matrices via left multiplication:
$e_1\cdot f = \sigma_1 f$: $e_1\leftrightarrow\sigma_x=\begin{pmatrix}0&1\\1&0\end{pmatrix}$
$e_2\cdot f = \sigma_2 f$: $e_2\leftrightarrow\sigma_y=\begin{pmatrix}0&{-i}\\i&0\end{pmatrix}$
$e_3\cdot f = \sigma_3 f$: $e_3\leftrightarrow\sigma_z=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&{-1}\end{pmatrix}$
Physical interpretation:
The 2-component Pauli spinor $\begin{pmatrix}\alpha\\\beta\end{pmatrix}$ is literally the element $\alpha f + \beta e_1 f\in S\subset\text{Cl}(3,0)$. No separate "spinor space" needed — spinors are already inside the algebra!
1x Period-8 pattern of Clifford algebra isomorphisms

Bott Periodicity

The period-8 theorem:
$\text{Cl}(n+8,0)\cong\text{Cl}(n,0)\otimes M_{16}(\mathbb{R})$

The sequence of real Clifford algebras repeats (up to matrix factors) with period 8.
The 8-fold table $\text{Cl}(n,0)$:
n=0: $\mathbb{R}$
n=1: $\mathbb{C}$
n=2: $\mathbb{H}$
n=3: $\mathbb{H}\oplus\mathbb{H}$
n=4: $M_2(\mathbb{H})$
n=5: $M_4(\mathbb{C})$
n=6: $M_8(\mathbb{R})$
n=7: $M_8(\mathbb{R})\oplus M_8(\mathbb{R})$
n=8: $M_{16}(\mathbb{R})$ — period restarts!
KO-theory connection:
Real K-theory $KO^{-n}(pt)\cong\pi_n(BO)$:
$\mathbb{Z}_2,\mathbb{Z}_2,0,\mathbb{Z},0,0,0,\mathbb{Z},\ldots$ (period 8).
Same pattern! Clifford algebras classify the Bott clock.
Topological insulators:
The 10-fold way (Altland–Zirnbauer) classifies free-fermion topological phases. The $\mathbb{Z}$ and $\mathbb{Z}_2$ entries in the periodic table come directly from the Bott clock of Clifford algebras (Kitaev 2009).

Conformal Geometry & Twistors

Conformal embedding:
$\text{Conf}(\mathbb{R}^{p,q})\hookrightarrow\text{Cl}(p{+}1,q{+}1)$

For Minkowski space $\mathbb{R}^{3,1}$:
$\text{Conf}(\mathbb{R}^{3,1}) = O(4,2)$
$\cong SU(2,2)/\mathbb{Z}_4$ — the twistor group!
Twistors:
A twistor $Z^\alpha$ is a Weyl spinor of $SU(2,2)\cong SO(4,2)$. In index notation: $Z^\alpha = (\omega^A, \pi_{A'})$, a pair of 2-component spinors satisfying the incidence relation $\omega^A = ix^{AA'}\pi_{A'}$.
Penrose's insight:
Massless particles (photons, gravitons) correspond to lines in twistor space $\mathbb{PT}\cong\mathbb{CP}^3$. A spacetime point $x$ maps to a line (Riemann sphere) in $\mathbb{PT}$ — the basic duality.
Möbius = conformal:
Möbius transformations $z\mapsto\frac{az+b}{cz+d}$ on $\mathbb{CP}^1\cong S^2$ are elements of $PSL(2,\mathbb{C})\cong SO^+(3,1)$ — the Lorentz group! They lift to $\text{Cl}(3,1)$ as sandwich products $X\mapsto MXM^\dagger$.
Amplituhedron connection:
Modern scattering amplitudes (Arkani-Hamed et al.) are computed as volumes of the amplituhedron in twistor space — a profound application of Penrose's original idea.
