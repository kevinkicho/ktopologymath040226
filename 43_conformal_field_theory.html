Conformal Maps

$w = f(z),\quad f'(z)\neq 0$

A holomorphic map with non-zero derivative is conformal: it preserves angles between curves at every point.

$\left|\frac{\partial w}{\partial x}\right|=\left|\frac{\partial w}{\partial y}\right|,\quad \frac{\partial w}{\partial x}\perp\frac{\partial w}{\partial y}$

Cauchy-Riemann equations ensure the Jacobian is a rotation+dilation — no shear.

The Conformal Group

In $d\geq 3$: finite-dimensional $SO(d+1,1)$ (dilations, translations, SCTs, rotations).

In $d=2$: infinite-dimensional! Every holomorphic function is locally conformal. The symmetry algebra is the Witt algebra with generators $\ell_n = -z^{n+1}\partial_z$, $n\in\mathbb{Z}$.

$[\ell_m,\ell_n] = (m-n)\ell_{m+n}$

Möbius / SL(2,ℂ)

$w = \frac{az+b}{cz+d},\quad ad-bc=1$

Maps circles and lines to circles and lines. The global conformal group of $\hat{\mathbb{C}}$ is $PSL(2,\mathbb{C})$.

Joukowski Map

$w = z + \frac{1}{z}$

Maps circles to airfoil cross-sections. Used in aerodynamics — the conformal image of a circle is a wing!

Worldsheet Connection

$w = \ln z$ "unrolls" a cylinder to a plane. The closed string worldsheet is conformally equivalent to $\mathbb{C}\setminus\{0\}$ — this is why CFT is the language of string theory.

Operator Product Expansion

$\mathcal{O}_i(z,\bar z)\,\mathcal{O}_j(w,\bar w)\sim\!\sum_k C_{ij}^k\,(z-w)^{h_k-h_i-h_j}\mathcal{O}_k(w)$

The OPE is the fundamental tool of CFT: as two local operators approach each other, their product is captured by an infinite sum over operators in the theory, with coefficients $C_{ij}^k$ (the OPE coefficients) and singular powers controlled by conformal dimensions.

Singularity Structure

When $h_k < h_i+h_j$: the OPE is singular (pole). The leading singularity as $z\to w$:

$\sim(z-w)^{h_k-h_i-h_j}$,  exponent $= h_k-h_i-h_j$

Non-integer exponent ⇒ branch cut. Integer exponent ⇒ pole. $h_k=h_i+h_j$ ⇒ logarithm.

Stress Tensor OPE

$T(z)\,T(w)\sim\dfrac{c/2}{(z-w)^4}+\dfrac{2T(w)}{(z-w)^2}+\dfrac{\partial T(w)}{z-w}$

This is the fundamental OPE of CFT. The $c/2$ pole encodes the conformal anomaly. It is equivalent to the Virasoro commutation relations.

T(z)·Primary

$T(z)\,\phi(w)\sim\dfrac{h\,\phi(w)}{(z-w)^2}+\dfrac{\partial\phi(w)}{z-w}$

This OPE defines a primary field of weight $h$. The $1/(z-w)^2$ pole gives the scaling dimension; the $1/(z-w)$ pole gives the derivative (infinitesimal translation).

Radial Quantization

OPEs encode equal-time commutators via the contour integral (contour = small circle around $w$):

Conformal Bootstrap

The four-point function of identical scalars $\langle\mathcal{O}(x_1)\cdots\mathcal{O}(x_4)\rangle$ depends only on cross-ratios $u,v$ via a conformal block decomposition.

$\mathcal{G}(u,v)=\sum_{\Delta,\ell}p_{\Delta,\ell}\,g_{\Delta,\ell}(u,v)$

Crossing Symmetry

Expanding in the s-channel (12→34) vs the t-channel (14→23) must give identical answers — this is the crossing equation:

$\sum_{\Delta,\ell}p_{\Delta,\ell}\left[v^{\Delta_\mathcal{O}}g_{\Delta,\ell}(u,v)-u^{\Delta_\mathcal{O}}g_{\Delta,\ell}(v,u)\right]=0$

Conformal Blocks

In 1D the scalar block (for the diagonal $z=\bar z$) is a hypergeometric function:

$g_\Delta(z)=z^\Delta\,{}_2F_1(\Delta,\Delta;2\Delta;z)$

In 2D the full block factorizes: $g_{\Delta,\ell}=k_{h}\,k_{\bar h}$ with $h=(\Delta+\ell)/2$, $\bar h=(\Delta-\ell)/2$ and $k_\beta(z)=z^\beta\,{}_2F_1(\beta,\beta;2\beta;z)$.

Solving the Bootstrap

The semidefinite programming (SDP) approach (Rattazzi-Rychkov-Tonni-Vichi, 2008) turns crossing symmetry into a convex optimization: apply linear functionals $\alpha$ such that $\alpha[F_{\Delta,\ell}]\geq 0$ for all allowed $\Delta,\ell$. This carves out the space of allowed CFT spectra — including exact results for the 3D Ising model ($\Delta_\sigma=0.5181489$).
