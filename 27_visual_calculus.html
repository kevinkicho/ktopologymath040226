Gradient ‚àáf ‚Äî steepest ascent
$\nabla f = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\hat{x} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\hat{y} + \frac{\partial f}{\partial z}\hat{z}$
Points in direction of maximum rate of increase. $|\nabla f|$ = rate of increase. $\nabla f \perp$ level curves. In physics: $\mathbf{E} = -\nabla\phi$ (electric field from potential).
Curl ‚àá√óF ‚Äî local rotation
$(\nabla\times\mathbf{F})_z = \frac{\partial F_y}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial F_x}{\partial y}$
Measures infinitesimal rotation. Irrotational: $\nabla\times\mathbf{F}=0$. Stokes' theorem: $\oint_C \mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{r} = \iint_S (\nabla\times\mathbf{F})\cdot d\mathbf{S}$.
Divergence ‚àá¬∑F ‚Äî source/sink strength
$\nabla\cdot\mathbf{F} = \frac{\partial F_x}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial F_y}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial F_z}{\partial z}$
Measures net outward flux per unit volume. Divergence theorem: $\oiint_S \mathbf{F}\cdot d\mathbf{S} = \iiint_V (\nabla\cdot\mathbf{F})\,dV$. Incompressible: $\nabla\cdot\mathbf{F}=0$.
Maxwell's Equations (‚àá form)
$\nabla\cdot\mathbf{E} = \rho/\varepsilon_0$   (Gauss, electric)
$\nabla\cdot\mathbf{B} = 0$   (Gauss, magnetic ‚Äî no monopoles)
$\nabla\times\mathbf{E} = -\partial\mathbf{B}/\partial t$   (Faraday)
$\nabla\times\mathbf{B} = \mu_0\mathbf{J} + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\,\partial\mathbf{E}/\partial t$   (Amp√®re-Maxwell)
Helmholtz Decomposition
Every smooth vector field $\mathbf{F}$ decomposes uniquely as $\mathbf{F} = -\nabla\phi + \nabla\times\mathbf{A}$ (irrotational + solenoidal). Fundamental to gauge theory and fluid mechanics.

Residue Theorem
$\oint_C f(z)\,dz = 2\pi i \sum_k \operatorname{Res}(f, z_k)$
Sum over poles $z_k$ inside contour $C$. Residue at simple pole: $\operatorname{Res}(f,z_0) = \lim_{z\to z_0}(z-z_0)f(z)$. For $1/(z^2+1)$: poles at $z=\pm i$, $\operatorname{Res}(f,i) = 1/(2i)$.
Cauchy's Integral Formula
$f^{(n)}(z_0) = \frac{n!}{2\pi i}\oint_C \frac{f(z)}{(z-z_0)^{n+1}}dz$
Values of holomorphic function determined entirely by boundary values. Implies all derivatives exist (analyticity).
Laurent Series
$f(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} a_n (z-z_0)^n$
The residue is $a_{-1}$, the coefficient of $(z-z_0)^{-1}$. Essential singularities have infinitely many negative-power terms (Picard's theorem).
Real Integral via Residues
$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\frac{dx}{x^2+1} = \pi$
Close contour in upper half-plane. Only $z=+i$ is enclosed. $\operatorname{Res}(f,i)=\frac{1}{2i}$, so integral $= 2\pi i \cdot \frac{1}{2i} = \pi$.
Bromwich Inversion (Laplace)
$f(t) = \frac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{\gamma-i\infty}^{\gamma+i\infty} e^{st} F(s)\,ds$
Inverse Laplace transform as contour integral in complex $s$-plane. Residues at poles of $F(s)$ give the time-domain response ‚Äî exponential modes $e^{s_k t}$.
Argument Principle
$\frac{1}{2\pi i}\oint_C \frac{f'(z)}{f(z)}dz = Z - P$ (zeros minus poles inside $C$). Basis of Nyquist stability criterion in control theory.

Key Theorems
Poincar√©-Bendixson:
A bounded trajectory in $\mathbb{R}^2$ with no fixed points must approach a closed orbit (limit cycle). Rules out chaos in 2D autonomous systems.
Hartman-Grobman (Linearization):
Near a hyperbolic fixed point $\mathbf{x}^*$, the nonlinear flow is topologically conjugate to the linearization $\dot{\xi} = D\mathbf{f}(\mathbf{x}^*)\xi$. Eigenvalues of Jacobian determine stability.
Lyapunov Stability:
If $\exists V(\mathbf{x}) > 0$ (Lyapunov function) with $\dot{V} \leq 0$ along trajectories, then $\mathbf{x}^*=0$ is stable. $\dot{V} < 0$ gives asymptotic stability.
Physical Applications
Van der Pol Oscillator:
$\ddot{x} - \mu(1-x^2)\dot{x} + x = 0$. Nonlinear damping: energy injected for $|x|<1$, dissipated for $|x|>1$. Stable limit cycle, used in cardiac pacemaker models.
RLC Circuit:
$L\ddot{q} + R\dot{q} + q/C = V(t)$. Same form as damped harmonic oscillator. Under/over/critically damped by $R^2$ vs $4L/C$.
SIR Epidemic:
$\dot{S}=-\beta SI$, $\dot{I}=\beta SI - \gamma I$. Threshold $R_0 = \beta S_0/\gamma$: epidemic spreads iff $R_0 > 1$. No endemic equilibrium in basic SIR.

Brachistochrone: find curve of fastest descent under gravity between two points.
Euler-Lagrange Equation
$J[y] = \int_a^b L(x, y, y')\,dx$
$\frac{d}{dx}\frac{\partial L}{\partial y'} - \frac{\partial L}{\partial y} = 0$
Necessary condition for $J[y]$ to be stationary. For brachistochrone $L = \sqrt{(1+y'^2)/(2gy)}$, solution is a cycloid: $x=r(\theta-\sin\theta)$, $y=r(1-\cos\theta)$.
Hamilton's Principle
$\delta S = \delta\int_{t_1}^{t_2}(T - V)\,dt = 0$
Action $S = \int L\,dt$ is stationary along physical trajectories. Euler-Lagrange equations $\equiv$ Newton's laws. Basis of quantum path integrals (Feynman).
Classical Problems
Dido's Isoperimetric Problem:
Among all closed curves of fixed perimeter $L$, the circle encloses maximum area $A = L^2/(4\pi)$. Proof via calculus of variations with Lagrange multiplier constraint.
Minimal Surfaces (Plateau's Problem):
Soap film minimizes area: $\nabla^2 z = 0$ for small deformations, mean curvature $H=0$ in general. Catenary $y=a\cosh(x/a)$ generates catenoid when rotated.
Pontryagin Maximum Principle:
Generalization to optimal control: $\dot{\mathbf{x}}=f(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{u})$, minimize $\int_0^T C(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{u})\,dt$. Costate/adjoint equations + Hamiltonian maximized over control $\mathbf{u}$.

Frenet-Serret Formulas
$\frac{d\mathbf{T}}{ds} = \kappa\,\mathbf{N}$
$\frac{d\mathbf{N}}{ds} = -\kappa\,\mathbf{T} + \tau\,\mathbf{B}$
$\frac{d\mathbf{B}}{ds} = -\tau\,\mathbf{N}$
$\mathbf{T}=$ unit tangent, $\mathbf{N}=$ principal normal, $\mathbf{B}=\mathbf{T}\times\mathbf{N}$ binormal. Curvature $\kappa = |d\mathbf{T}/ds|$ measures bending. Torsion $\tau$ measures twisting out of the osculating plane.
Fundamental Theorem of Curves
Given smooth $\kappa(s)>0$ and $\tau(s)$, there exists a unique (up to rigid motion) curve with these curvature and torsion functions. Curves are completely characterized by $(\kappa(s), \tau(s))$.
Topology & Applications
Four-Vertex Theorem:
Every smooth closed convex curve in $\mathbb{R}^2$ has at least 4 vertices (extrema of curvature). Generalizes to non-convex curves (Mukhopadhyaya).
Total Curvature:
$\int_C \kappa\,ds = 2\pi\,n$ for a closed plane curve with winding number $n$ (Hopf Umlaufsatz). For a space curve, $\int\kappa\,ds \geq 2\pi$ (Fenchel's theorem).
DNA Supercoiling:
Linking number $Lk = Tw + Wr$ (White's formula). Writhe $Wr$ measures spatial geometry of DNA axis (Frenet torsion contribution); twist $Tw$ counts helical turns of double helix. Topoisomerases change $Lk$.
Elastica (Euler's Elastic Curves):
Minimize $\int \kappa^2\,ds$ subject to length constraint. Solutions are elliptic integrals ‚Äî describe buckled rods, shape of red blood cells.
