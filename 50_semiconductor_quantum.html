Quantum Confinement

When a semiconductor dimension shrinks below the de Broglie wavelength (~10 nm for electrons in Si), quantum mechanics forces energy levels to become discrete.

$$E_n = \frac{n^2\pi^2\hbar^2}{2m^*L^2}$$

$m^* \approx 0.19\,m_e$ for electrons in Si. As $L$ decreases, $E_n \propto 1/L^2$ — levels spread apart rapidly.

Confinement Geometries

  • Quantum Well: confined in 1D (thin slab), free in 2D. Density of states is a staircase.
  • Quantum Wire: confined in 2D, free in 1D (GAA channel). Density of states has 1D van Hove singularities.

Wavefunction

$$\psi_n(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}}\sin\!\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)$$

Standing waves vanishing at the walls. Higher $n$ → more nodes → higher kinetic energy.

Density of States

Left panel shows the 2D step-function DOS for a quantum well, or the 1D spike (van Hove) DOS for a wire. Each step/spike marks a new subband becoming accessible.

GAA transistors exploit 1D confinement at the 2nm node.

Gate-All-Around (GAA)

A nanosheet transistor where the gate wraps all 4 sides of the silicon channel (~3 nm thick, ~20 nm wide). Superior electrostatic control suppresses short-channel effects.

Why It Matters

  • FinFET: gate on 3 sides → some leakage
  • GAA: gate on all 4 → near-ideal subthreshold slope
  • Subthreshold swing approaches $60\,\mathrm{mV/dec}$ limit

Band Bending

$$\phi_s \propto V_G - V_{th}$$

Gate voltage bends the conduction band. Above $V_{th}$, the channel inverts — electrons accumulate and current flows.

Moore's Law Context

  • Intel 10 nm FinFET (2019)
  • TSMC 3 nm FinFET (2022)
  • Samsung 3 nm GAA (2022)
  • TSMC 2 nm GAA (2025)
  • Intel 18A GAA (2025)

At 2 nm, the channel is only ~10 silicon atoms across.

Larmor Precession

$$\omega_L = \frac{eB}{m_e}$$

An electron spin precesses around an external magnetic field $\mathbf{B}$ at the Larmor frequency. This is the basis of NMR, MRI, and spintronic devices.

Giant Magnetoresistance

$$\frac{\Delta R}{R} = \frac{2P^2}{1-P^2}$$

$P$ = spin polarization of the ferromagnet. Discovered by Fert and Grünberg (1988).

Two-Current Model

Spin-↑ and spin-↓ electrons are independent channels. In parallel alignment, one channel passes easily → low total resistance. In antiparallel, both channels scatter → high resistance.

Applications

  • Hard drive read heads (since ~1997)
  • Enabled terabyte-scale magnetic storage
  • Magnetic field sensors

2007 Nobel Prize: Albert Fert & Peter Grünberg

Magnetic Tunnel Junction

A thin insulating barrier (MgO, ~1 nm) sandwiched between two ferromagnetic layers. Electrons tunnel through the barrier; rate depends on spin alignment.

Tunneling Magnetoresistance

$$\mathrm{TMR} = \frac{R_{AP}-R_P}{R_P} \approx \frac{2P^2}{1-P^2}$$

$P$ = spin polarization. TMR ratios of 600% achieved in MgO-based junctions.

Spin-Hall Effect

A charge current $J_c$ through a heavy metal (Pt, Ta, W) generates a transverse spin current $J_s$ via spin-orbit coupling:

$$\mathbf{J}_s = \theta_\mathrm{SH}\frac{\hbar}{2e}\hat{z}\times\mathbf{J}_c$$

This spin current exerts a torque on the adjacent free layer magnetization, driving fast deterministic switching.

Memory Operation

  • State 0: Free ∥ Fixed → $R_P$ (low) → "0"
  • State 1: Free ↑↓ Fixed → $R_{AP}$ (high) → "1"

Current pulse above threshold switches the free layer.

SOT-MRAM: non-volatile, fast (~ns), endurance >10¹² cycles.

Quantum Well & 2DEG

A GaAs layer (~10 nm) sandwiched between AlGaAs barriers confines electrons in 1D. Free motion in the 2D plane creates a two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG).

$$E_n = \frac{n^2\pi^2\hbar^2}{2m^*L^2}, \quad \psi_n(z)=\sqrt{\frac{2}{L}}\sin\!\frac{n\pi z}{L}$$

Effective Mass & k·p Theory

Near a band extremum the dispersion is parabolic: $E(\mathbf{k})=\hbar^2k^2/2m^*$. k·p perturbation theory gives $m^*$ from band mixing (GaAs: $m^*=0.067m_e$).

2DEG Density of States

$$g_{2D}(E) = \frac{m^*}{\pi\hbar^2}\sum_n \Theta(E-E_n)$$

Constant DOS per subband → staircase. Each occupied subband contributes $n_{2D}^{(n)}=g_{2D}\cdot(E_F-E_n)$ electrons.

Band Offset Types

  • Type I: Both CB & VB offsets confine carriers to same layer (GaAs/AlGaAs). Ideal for 2DEG.
  • Type II: Staggered — electrons & holes in different layers (InAs/GaSb).

HEMT

High Electron Mobility Transistor: 2DEG remotely doped → donors in AlGaAs, electrons in GaAs, spatially separated from impurities. Mobility $\mu>10^7$ cm²/Vs at 4 K.

Precursor to quantum Hall effect. Used in every cellphone low-noise amplifier.

Quantum Hall Effect

2DEG in a strong perpendicular B field. Cyclotron motion quantizes energy into Landau levels:

$$E_N = \hbar\omega_c\!\left(N+\tfrac{1}{2}\right),\quad \omega_c=\frac{eB}{m^*}$$

Each Landau level holds $n_B = eB/h$ states per unit area (degeneracy). As B sweeps, $E_F$ jumps between LLs → Hall plateaus.

von Klitzing Constant

$$R_{xy} = \frac{h}{\nu e^2} = \frac{R_K}{\nu},\quad R_K = 25812.807\,\Omega$$

$\nu$ is an integer (IQHE) or simple fraction (FQHE). Topological protection via TKNN invariant (Chern number) makes this universal to 1 ppb.

Edge States

Bulk is insulating; chiral edge channels carry current without backscattering → $R_{xx}=0$ at plateaus. Number of edge channels = filling factor $\nu$.

Fractional QHE

  • $\nu=1/3, 2/5, 3/7, \ldots$ — composite fermions (Jain, 1989)
  • $\nu=5/2$ — Moore–Read (Pfaffian) state, non-Abelian anyons
  • Braiding non-Abelian anyons = fault-tolerant gate (topological QC)

1985 Nobel: von Klitzing. 1998 Nobel: Laughlin, Störmer, Tsui.

Singlet–Triplet Qubit

Two electrons in a double quantum dot (DQD). Qubit states:

$$|S\rangle=\frac{|\!\uparrow\downarrow\rangle-|\!\downarrow\uparrow\rangle}{\sqrt{2}},\quad |T_0\rangle=\frac{|\!\uparrow\downarrow\rangle+|\!\downarrow\uparrow\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}$$

Exchange Coupling

$$J(\varepsilon)=\frac{4t_c^2}{U-\varepsilon},\quad H=\frac{J}{2}\sigma_z + \frac{\Delta B_z}{2}\sigma_x$$

Detuning $\varepsilon$ tunes $J$: large positive $\varepsilon$ → large $J$ → fast $|S\rangle$-$|T_0\rangle$ splitting (Z-rotation). $\Delta B_z$ (nuclear gradient or micromagnet) drives X-rotation.

Pauli Spin Blockade

Triplet (1,1) → (0,2) transition forbidden by Pauli exclusion. Enables single-shot spin readout via current measurement.

Material Platforms

  • GaAs/AlGaAs: first demonstrations (2005), limited by ²⁷Al, ⁶⁹Ga, ⁷¹Ga nuclear bath. $T_2^*\sim 10$ ns.
  • Si/SiGe & ²⁸Si: isotopic purification eliminates nuclear spin → $T_2^*>100$ ns, $T_2>1$ ms with dynamical decoupling.

Milestones

  • Loss–DiVincenzo proposal: 1998
  • Single-spin readout (Elzerman): 2004
  • 2-qubit CNOT fidelity >99%: 2022 (Intel/Delft)
  • Crossbar array architectures: ongoing (Intel, QuTech)

Topological Insulators

Bulk band gap opened by strong spin-orbit coupling (SOC), but topologically protected helical surface states (spin locked to momentum) cross the gap.

$$H_\mathrm{surface} = \hbar v_F\,(\mathbf{k}\times\hat{z})\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}$$

Time-reversal symmetry (TRS) protects surface Dirac cone from backscattering. $Z_2$ topological invariant classifies the bulk: trivial ($\nu=0$) vs topological ($\nu=1$).

Materials

  • Bi₂Se₃, Bi₂Te₃ — ARPES confirmed single Dirac cone
  • HgTe quantum wells (2D TI, QSHE) — König et al. 2007

Kitaev Chain & Majorana

$$H=-\mu\sum_i c_i^\dagger c_i - t\sum_i(c_i^\dagger c_{i+1}+\mathrm{h.c.}) + \Delta\sum_i(c_ic_{i+1}+\mathrm{h.c.})$$

Topological phase ($|\mu|<2t$): Majorana zero modes appear at chain ends. Not electrons — self-conjugate quasiparticles $\gamma=\gamma^\dagger$.

Towards Topological QC

  • Fu–Kane model: TI surface + s-wave SC → effective TSC
  • Zero-bias conductance peak: MBS signature in InAs/Al nanowires
  • Non-Abelian braiding → fault-tolerant qubit gates
  • Microsoft Station Q & topological qubit program (2023–)

2016 Nobel (Thouless, Haldane, Kosterlitz) for topological phases.

Coulomb Blockade

A quantum dot is so small ($C \sim$ aF) that adding one electron costs charging energy $E_C=e^2/2C \gg k_BT$. Current is blocked unless $V_g$ aligns a level with the Fermi energy.

$$E(N) = \frac{(Ne)^2}{2C} - NeV_g, \quad E_C = \frac{e^2}{2C}$$

Artificial Atom

Shell structure with Hund's rule filling: electrons obey same rules as atomic orbitals but with tunable parameters. Shell closings at N=2,6,12 observed in vertical QDs.

Kondo Effect

Odd-N QD has net spin-½; below $T_K$ (Kondo temperature) it forms a spin singlet with reservoir electrons → enhanced conductance at $V_{SD}=0$ (Kondo ridge).

Optical Quantum Dots

  • InAs/GaAs self-assembled QDs — single-photon sources, $g^{(2)}(0)\approx 0$
  • Purcell factor $F_P>100$ in photonic crystal cavities
  • QD-LED displays: high color purity (FWHM <20 nm)
  • Spin-photon interface for quantum networks

Loss–DiVincenzo Qubit

Single spin in QD as qubit. Exchange gate via tunnel coupling between adjacent dots. Readout via Pauli spin blockade or rf-gate sensing.

QD single-photon sources: key component for photonic quantum computing.
