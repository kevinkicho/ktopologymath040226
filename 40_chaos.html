Poincaré Section

A Poincaré section is a stroboscopic snapshot: sample $(θ, \dot{θ})$ once per drive period. Integrable orbits $\Rightarrow$ closed curves (KAM tori). Chaos $\Rightarrow$ scattered cloud.
KAM theory: as $A$ grows, rational tori break into Birkhoff island chains first (Poincaré-Birkhoff theorem), surrounded by thin chaotic layers (homoclinic tangles). Irrational tori survive longest (noble numbers).
Arnold diffusion: in $\geq 3$ DOF systems, chaotic regions are connected — trajectories can wander through all of phase space, even if KAM tori block it in 2 DOF.

KAM Theory & Standard Map

Chirikov Standard Map: $$p_{n+1} = p_n + K\sin(q_n)$$ $$q_{n+1} = q_n + p_{n+1} \pmod{2\pi}$$ Area-preserving (Hamiltonian). Derived from a kicked rotor.
KAM Theorem: For small $K$, most invariant tori of the integrable system persist as quasiperiodic orbits (closed curves). Rational tori break first → Birkhoff island chains.

Chirikov criterion: global chaos at $K\gtrsim0.9716$. The last KAM torus to break has winding number equal to the golden ratio.
Arnold diffusion: in ≥3 DOF, chaotic regions connect across all of phase space even when 2D tori block transport locally.

Quantum Chaos

BGS conjecture (Bohigas, Giannoni, Schmit 1984): energy level statistics of quantum systems whose classical counterparts are chaotic follow random matrix theory.

Integrable: Poisson spacing $P(s)=e^{-s}$
Chaotic: Wigner-Dyson $P(s)=\frac{\pi s}{2}e^{-\pi s^2/4}$ (GUE)
Random Matrix Theory: eigenvalues of large random Hermitian matrices (GUE) show level repulsion — consecutive levels almost never coincide. This matches chaotic quantum spectra.
OTOC (out-of-time-order correlator): $$C(t)=-\langle[W(t),V]^2\rangle$$ Grows as $\sim e^{\lambda_L t}$ until scrambling time. $\lambda_L\leq 2\pi k_BT/\hbar$ (Maldacena-Shenker-Susskind bound). Saturates at $\sim 1/N$ — information scrambled across all degrees of freedom.
Quantum kicked rotor: dynamical localization suppresses classical diffusion $\langle p^2\rangle\sim Dt$ → quantum saturation at localization length $\ell\sim D/\hbar^2$.
