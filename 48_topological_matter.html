Quantum Hall Effect

In a 2D electron gas at low temperature and strong magnetic field, the Hall conductance is precisely quantized:

$$\sigma_{xy} = n\frac{e^2}{h}$$

The integer $n$ is a topological invariant — the Chern number — immune to disorder.

Landau Levels

$$E_n = \hbar\omega_c\!\left(n+\tfrac{1}{2}\right),\quad \omega_c = \frac{eB}{m}$$

Electrons in a magnetic field form discrete energy levels. When the Fermi energy lies between levels, $\sigma_{xy}$ is on a plateau.

Filling Factor

$$\nu = \frac{nh}{eB}$$

Chern Number (TKNN)

$$C = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{\mathrm{BZ}} \Omega_n\,d^2k$$

The Berry curvature $\Omega_n$ integrated over the Brillouin zone gives an integer. This is the topological origin of quantization.

Fractional QHE

At filling $\nu = 1/3, 2/5, \ldots$ strong electron–electron interactions produce a new topological state with fractionally charged quasi-particles (anyons).

Berry Phase & Holonomy

$$\gamma_n = i\oint_C \langle u_k|\nabla_{\!k}|u_k\rangle\cdot dk$$

A geometric phase accumulated when a Bloch state $|u_k\rangle$ is adiabatically transported around a closed loop $C$ in the Brillouin zone. It is gauge-invariant for closed loops.

$$\boldsymbol{\mathcal{A}}_k = i\langle u_k|\nabla_k u_k\rangle\quad\text{(Berry connection)}$$
$$\boldsymbol{\Omega} = \nabla_k\times\boldsymbol{\mathcal{A}}\quad\text{(Berry curvature)}$$

For a spin-1/2 in a magnetic field, a Dirac monopole at the origin of parameter space sources the curvature. The Berry phase equals half the solid angle subtended on the Bloch sphere:

$$\gamma = \frac{\Omega_{\mathrm{solid}}}{2}$$

A non-contractible loop enclosing the monopole gives $\gamma = \pi$ — a sign flip of the wavefunction, the topological signature of Chern number $= 1$.

Holonomy

The spin vector on the Bloch sphere is parallel-transported along the loop. After completing the circuit it returns rotated by $\gamma$ — holonomy in the fiber bundle sense (Penrose §15.4).

TKNN Invariant

Thouless, Kohmoto, Nightingale & den Nijs (1982) showed the Hall conductance equals a topological integer:

$$C = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{\mathrm{BZ}} \Omega_{xy}(\mathbf{k})\,d^2k \in \mathbb{Z}$$

Berry Curvature

$$\Omega_{xy} = -2\,\mathrm{Im}\!\left\langle\frac{\partial u}{\partial k_x}\Bigg|\frac{\partial u}{\partial k_y}\right\rangle$$

Acts as a "magnetic field" in k-space. Sources/sinks are Weyl-point monopoles. Color map: red = positive, blue = negative curvature.

Haldane Model

A honeycomb lattice with complex next-nearest-neighbor hopping $t_2 e^{i\phi}$ can have $C = \pm 1$ without net flux — the first realization of a Chern insulator.

$$H(\mathbf{k}) = \mathbf{d}(\mathbf{k})\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}$$

Phase transition at $|M| = 3\sqrt{3}\,t_2|\sin\phi|$: gap closes at a Dirac point where $\Omega$ diverges.

Bulk–Boundary

$C$ counts the net number of chiral edge modes. Each carries conductance $e^2/h$, giving the quantized plateau.

Z₂ Topological Insulators

Time-reversal symmetry $\mathcal{T}^2 = -1$ (spin-1/2) forces Kramers degeneracy at time-reversal invariant momenta (TRIM): $\Gamma, M, X$.

$$\nu = \prod_{i\in\mathrm{TRIM}} \frac{\sqrt{\det[\mathbf{w}(\Lambda_i)]}}{\mathrm{Pf}[\mathbf{w}(\Lambda_i)]} = 0,1$$

Kane-Mele Model

Graphene with spin-orbit coupling: intrinsic SOC opens a gap without breaking TR. The $\mathbb{Z}_2$ invariant $\nu = 1$ when SOC exceeds Rashba coupling.

$$H = t\sum_{\langle ij\rangle}c^\dagger_i c_j + i\lambda_{\rm SO}\sum_{\langle\langle ij\rangle\rangle}\nu_{ij}c^\dagger_i s_z c_j$$

Topological Protection

Edge modes are helical: spin-up moves right, spin-down moves left. Backscattering requires a spin flip — forbidden by TR symmetry. Disorder cannot open a gap in the edge spectrum.

Kramers Pairs

At TRIM points, edge states come in degenerate pairs. They cannot hybridize without breaking TR, guaranteeing gapless crossings.

Giant Magnetoresistance

In alternating ferromagnet / normal-metal / ferromagnet stacks, resistance depends strongly on the relative magnetic alignment.

$$\frac{\Delta R}{R} = \frac{2P^2}{1-P^2}$$

where $P$ = spin polarization of the ferromagnet. Discovered by Fert (Fe/Cr, 1988) and independently by Grünberg.

Two-Current Model

Spin-up and spin-down electrons are independent channels. In the parallel configuration, one channel passes easily (low $\rho$) and one is scattered (high $\rho$) — overall low resistance. In antiparallel, both channels are scattered — high resistance.

Applications

  • Hard drive read heads (enabled terabyte drives)
  • Magnetic field sensors
  • MRAM (magnetic RAM)
  • Biosensors

Hard Drive Bit Reading

The read head scans magnetic domains. Domain walls cause transitions in resistance $R_P \leftrightarrow R_{AP}$, encoding bits 0 and 1.

2007 Nobel Prize: Albert Fert & Peter Grünberg
